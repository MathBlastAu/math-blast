# The Crystal Compass — Year 2 Arc Brief

**Target audience:** Year 2 girls, ~7 years old  
**Setting:** Methodist Ladies' College, Perth — classroom delivery, hybrid model  
**Teacher:** Miss Zara (Izzy's teacher)  
**Delivery:** Teacher plays on board, class discusses, kids do quiz individually  
**Writing rules:** Max 10–12 words per sentence. Simple vocabulary. Short story blocks.

---

## Arc Premise

Four girls find a magical compass. It only works when they solve maths puzzles together. Each issue, the compass leads them somewhere new — but only if they use their maths skills and the values they believe in.

## Issue 1 — Place Value: "The Compass Wakes"

**MLC Value:** Integrity (things must be in their right place)  
**Y2 Maths:** Place value — tens and ones (numbers to 100)  
**Story length:** ~6 short paragraphs  

### Story Beats
1. The four girls find a dusty old box in the library. Inside is a glowing compass.
2. The compass shows a number — but the digits are scrambled. It won't work until they fix it.
3. Zoe says: "The numbers have to be in the right place. That's how it works."
4. Together they learn that 10 ones make a ten. They fix the compass.
5. The compass glows gold and points to a door. Adventure begins.
6. **Cliffhanger:** The door opens to reveal a dark forest full of glowing lights.

### Quiz Questions (10)
1. How many ones are in the number 34? (4)
2. How many tens are in the number 34? (3)
3. What is 2 tens and 7 ones? (27)
4. What is 5 tens and 0 ones? (50)
5. Which is bigger — 47 or 74? (74)
6. What number has 6 tens and 3 ones? (63)
7. Put 8 tens and 1 one together. What number is it? (81)
8. 52 — how many tens? (5)
9. 52 — how many ones? (2)
10. What comes between 39 and 41? (40)

### Image Scenes
- Opening: four girls peering into a dusty box, warm library light
- Compass glowing gold in Zoe's hands
- Tens-and-ones visual (stacked blocks: 3 towers of 10 + 4 singles = 34)
- Door opening to glowing forest — cliffhanger

## Issue 2 — Skip Counting: "The Forest of Patterns"

**MLC Value:** Wonder (patterns are everywhere)  
**Y2 Maths:** Skip counting by 2s, 5s, and 10s  
**Story length:** ~6 short paragraphs  

### Story Beats
1. The girls enter the glowing forest. The lights pulse in patterns.
2. Priya notices: "The lights flash every 2 steps. It's a pattern!"
3. They follow the pattern deeper into the forest. Skip by 2s first, then 5s.
4. A river blocks their path. Stepping stones are numbered — skip by 10s to cross.
5. The compass lights up again. They're getting closer.
6. **Cliffhanger:** A giant stone door. It has a pattern lock — what comes next?

### Quiz Questions (10)
1. Count by 2s: 2, 4, 6, __, 10 (8)
2. Count by 2s: 14, 16, __, 20 (18)
3. Count by 5s: 5, 10, __, 20 (15)
4. Count by 5s: 25, 30, __, 40 (35)
5. Count by 10s: 10, 20, __, 40 (30)
6. Count by 10s: 50, 60, __, 80 (70)
7. What pattern is 4, 6, 8, 10? (count by 2s)
8. What comes next: 15, 20, 25, __? (30)
9. Count by 2s from 0 to 10 — how many jumps? (5)
10. Count by 10s: 30, __, 50 (40)

### Image Scenes
- Glowing forest with lights pulsing in rows of 2
- Priya pointing at the pattern, excited
- Stepping stones across a river numbered by 10s
- Stone door with a pattern carved in it — cliffhanger

## Issue 3 — Addition & Subtraction: "The Courage Climb"

**MLC Value:** Courage (tackling big challenges)  
**Y2 Maths:** Addition and subtraction to 100  
**Story length:** ~6 short paragraphs  

### Story Beats
1. The stone door opens. There's a tall mountain. The compass points up.
2. Isla says: "We have to climb. I'll go first." She's scared but she goes anyway.
3. Each step has a number puzzle. Get it right and the step holds firm.
4. Amara slips. Isla reaches back and helps her up. Courage isn't just for yourself.
5. They reach the top. The view is amazing. The compass spins fast.
6. **Cliffhanger:** Below them, they see a village — and it's in trouble.

### Quiz Questions (10)
1. 23 + 14 = ? (37)
2. 45 + 30 = ? (75)
3. 61 + 18 = ? (79)
4. 50 – 20 = ? (30)
5. 78 – 15 = ? (63)
6. 34 + 46 = ? (80)
7. 90 – 27 = ? (63)
8. 55 + 25 = ? (80)
9. 100 – 38 = ? (62)
10. 47 + 36 = ? (83)

### Image Scenes
- Mountain path with number puzzles on each step
- Isla reaching her hand back to Amara on the cliff
- View from the top — four girls, compass glowing
- Village below with a problem — cliffhanger

## Issue 4 — Measurement: "The Village Needs Help"

**MLC Value:** Humanity (sharing fairly, caring for others)  
**Y2 Maths:** Measurement — length, mass, capacity  
**Story length:** ~6 short paragraphs  

### Story Beats
1. The girls climb down. The village has a big problem — things are the wrong size.
2. Bridges are too short. Buckets are too heavy. Bags won't fit.
3. Amara says: "We need to measure. Then we can fix it."
4. Each girl uses measurement to solve one problem for the village.
5. The villagers cheer. The compass glows bright white — the brightest yet.
6. **Ending:** The compass shows the way home. The girls know they'll come back.

### Quiz Questions (10)
1. Which is longer — 15 cm or 20 cm? (20 cm)
2. Which is heavier — 3 kg or 1 kg? (3 kg)
3. A rope is 12 cm. Another is 8 cm. How long together? (20 cm)
4. A jar holds 5 cups. You pour in 3. How many more fit? (2)
5. Order from shortest to longest: 7 cm, 2 cm, 10 cm (2, 7, 10)
6. A plank is 30 cm. You cut off 12 cm. What's left? (18 cm)
7. Which holds more — a bucket or a cup? (bucket)
8. Mia is 90 cm tall. Zoe is 85 cm tall. How much taller is Mia? (5 cm)
9. A bag can hold 10 kg. You put in 6 kg. How much space left? (4 kg)
10. Two planks: 14 cm and 16 cm. How long together? (30 cm)

### Image Scenes
- Village with wonky bridges and oversized/undersized objects
- Amara measuring a bridge with a tape measure
- All four girls helping different villagers
- Final scene: compass glowing white, girls waving goodbye
