🌏 Chapter 1 — Everyone, Together

One Hundred and Twenty Sprockets. All Five Villages.

Tangle had led them all home. Every missing Sprocket — from the Deep Root, from the narrow paths, from the far side of Split Ridge. Vine by vine, patient as it had always been, it had rebuilt every bridge it had taken down. And now, for the first time in centuries, all five villages stood together in a single clearing.

One hundred and twenty Sprockets. Five villages: Branchwick, Fernmoss, the Cascade Clearing, the Array Forest settlement, and Split Ridge. Chief Pip stood at the front, antenna vibrating with emotion. Tangle stood at the back — enormous, still, watching.

The first order of business: the food store. Three hundred and sixty items, to be shared equally among all one hundred and twenty Sprockets.

🏘️ Chapter 2 — Village by Village

Fair Doesn't Always Mean Equal Numbers.

Press ▶ to hear the story
Three items each was the starting point. But Chief Pip had a harder question: each village had a different number of Sprockets. If the food was truly fair, each village should receive an amount proportional to its size — not just an equal number.

Fernmoss had twenty-four Sprockets — one fifth of the total population. So Fernmoss should receive one fifth of the food. Split Ridge had thirty Sprockets — one quarter of the total. And so on.

"This is what fair really means," Chief Pip said quietly. "Not the same for everyone. The right amount for everyone."

🌿 Chapter 3 — Tangle Builds the Bridges

The Best Use of a Gift Is for Others.

Press ▶ to hear the story
While the food was being distributed, Tangle was already working. It had counted the broken bridges: five in total, each needing forty-eight vines. Two hundred and forty vines exactly. Tangle had two hundred and forty. A perfect match — no remainder.

Then came the timeline. The village builders could complete three bridges per day. Five bridges across two days — three on day one, two on day two. Not perfectly equal across the days, but the work would be done.

Blaze watched Tangle carry the vine bundles in perfect groups of twelve. "You've been planning this," she said. It didn't answer. But it had.

💧 Chapter 4 — The Dry Season

The Hardest Division of All.

Press ▶ to hear the story
The Dry Season was coming. Every Sprocket knew it — the weeks when the jungle went silent and the water pods were all that kept the tribe alive. Chief Pip pulled out the ledger. Five hundred and four water pods for seven villages. The distribution had to be perfect.

Each village's share would need to last four months. Branchwick had five families who would need to divide their monthly share between them. At every level, the same question: how many for each?

"Division keeps us alive," Chief Pip said. "It always has."

🏆 Chapter 5 — Tangle's Final Act

The Shape of Everything It Learned.

Press ▶ to hear the story
When the distribution was done, Chief Pip made a calculation. Blaze helped. All five village shares added to three hundred and sixty — the total. Fernmoss seventy-two, Split Ridge ninety, Cascade fifty-four, Array Forest sixty-three, Branchwick eighty-one. Every item accounted for. Not equal amounts — but fair ones. Proportional. And the sum was perfect.

Then Tangle did something no one expected. It began moving the Sprockets. Not roughly — precisely. Guiding each one gently to a position, then the next, then the next. Row by row. When it was finished, four hundred and eighty Sprockets stood in a perfect rectangular array on the jungle floor.

Thirty rows of sixteen. A multiplication array. And from above, anyone could see what it was: the same pattern as Tangle's sculptures, but made of living things. Tangle had arranged the whole reunion into a piece of mathematics.

Chief Pip stood at the front of the array — thirty rows of sixteen, every Sprocket in the Verdant Canopy standing perfectly still.

"Tangle," he said, loud enough for all to hear, "you built us a number line. You counted every vine. You organised us into an array. You have been doing mathematics your whole life, and you have done more for this tribe today than any Sprocket alive."

He extended his tiny antenna toward the enormous vine creature at the back.

"By unanimous decision — you are an Honorary Sprocket. Welcome home."

Tangle set down a bundle of twelve vines. And for the first time, it wasn't a question. It wasn't confusion. It was a gift.
