💧 Chapter 1 — A Festival Once in a Century

Everything Must Be Grouped — Not Shared!

Press ▶ to hear the story
The path from Fernmoss led Blaze through the mist — and suddenly the trees opened up to reveal Cascade Clearing: a wide, misty waterfall clearing in the middle-canopy, glowing with soft rainbow light.

The Splash-Sprockets were everywhere, rushing in every direction. A century-old festival was about to begin — and nothing was ready.

"You! Explorer! Can you count in groups?" called a small Sprocket with a silver stripe on each antenna — the Elder. "Everything for the festival must be arranged in exact group sizes. Not shared. Grouped. We have sixty lanterns and they must go in groups of ten."

🌿 Chapter 3 — Tangle's Bundles

Someone Has Been Here Before Us...

Press ▶ to hear the story
While Blaze worked, a Splash-Sprocket named Ripple came running over, breathless.

"Explorer! There's something strange near the waterfall — a huge pile of vines, all bundled up. Eighty-four of them, in neat groups of twelve."

Blaze went to look. The bundles were perfect — seven of them, identical. This was not random. She found a second cache deeper in the clearing: ninety-six vines, arranged in groups of six.

"It's organising," Blaze said quietly. "Not destroying. Grouping — just like us."

👁️ Chapter 5 — Blaze Wins. Tangle Watches.

Something at the Edge of the Trees...

Press ▶ to hear the story
Blaze had answered every challenge. The Elder nodded slowly — the closest thing a Splash-Sprocket did to applause.

"One final test. One hundred and fifty-six sparkle-stones. Groups of twelve. How many groups?"

Then the Elder raised one more question — the hardest yet. Two hundred sparkle-stones, laid out in a long row. The Elder wanted exactly twenty-five groups. But the group size was unknown.

"Work backwards," Blaze said. "Two hundred divided by twenty-five tells you the group size."
