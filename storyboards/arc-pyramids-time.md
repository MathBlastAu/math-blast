# Arc Storyboard: Pyramids — Time
## "The Chronos Tombs"

**World:** The Desert of Vel'Nara — an ancient desert on a distant planet with massive pyramids, underground chambers, solar clocks, and a civilisation that measured everything in time  
**Math Topic:** Time (clock reading, elapsed time, duration, timetables, 24-hour time, calendar calculations)  
**ACARA Content:** AC9M3M01, AC9M2M01 → AC9M4M02, AC9M4M03 (Year 3 → Year 4)  
**Issues:** 7  
**Character Choice:** Jake (methodical, plans the schedule in advance) or Blaze (instinctive, moves fast but miscalculates)

---

## Arc Overview

### The Big Story
The **Vel'Nara** were an ancient civilisation of pyramid-builders who were obsessed with time. They built everything around precise time calculations — doors that only open at exact moments, traps that trigger at specific intervals, ceremonies that must happen at the right time or fail forever. Their civilisation was destroyed when they lost track of time: their Master Clock broke, two factions disagreed about what time it was, and they couldn't coordinate. Their whole civilisation froze.

Jake/Blaze arrives as a junior archaeologist/explorer and enters the Chronos Tombs — a series of seven pyramids, each built for a different aspect of time. To move through each pyramid, they must solve the time locks. The seventh pyramid holds the Master Clock — and if Jake/Blaze can fix it, the Vel'Nara civilisation might be restored.

### Villain/Antagonist  
**Professor Tempo** — a rival archaeologist who is obsessed with the Vel'Nara mystery and believes that time puzzles must be solved by gut instinct, not calculation. Tempo keeps getting things wrong (adding time incorrectly, misreading clocks) and accidentally triggering traps that put both of them at risk. Not malicious — just overconfident. By Issue 7, Tempo is trapped in the Master Clock chamber, and Jake/Blaze must solve one final time puzzle to free him.

### How Time Threads Through the Arc
- **Issues 1–2:** Reading analogue clocks (to 5 minutes), simple elapsed time, adding time intervals
- **Issues 3–4:** Elapsed time with open number lines, converting between units (minutes/hours)
- **Issues 5–6:** Timetables, 24-hour time, multi-step time problems
- **Issue 7:** Calendar calculations, time zones (the Vel'Nara used a different calendar), fixing the Master Clock

### Jake vs Blaze Personality Differences
| Aspect | Jake | Blaze |
|--------|------|-------|
| Approach | Draws an open number line for every elapsed time problem | Estimates first ("about an hour") then refines |
| Time reading | Traces the clock hands methodically | Reads it at a glance, sometimes gets minutes/hours mixed up |
| Scheduling | Plans the day's activities out in advance | Adjusts on the fly — "we have more time than I thought" |
| Relationship with Tempo | Careful — Tempo always makes Jake more precise by contrast | Exasperated — "You did it wrong AGAIN, Professor" |

## Issue 1 of 7 — "The Pyramid of Hours"

**Title:** The Pyramid of Hours  
**Setting:** The First Pyramid (Pyramid of Khu) — its walls are covered in clock faces; everything opens and closes on the hour  
**Difficulty:** Year 3 entry — reading analogue clocks to 5 minutes, o'clock, quarter-past, half-past, quarter-to

### Story Premise
Jake/Blaze enters the Pyramid of Khu. Every door opens at a specific time shown on a stone clock face — and only stays open for 10 minutes. Jake/Blaze must read each clock correctly and move through before the window closes. Professor Tempo is already inside and has been stuck at the second door for 20 minutes because he misread quarter-past as quarter-to.

**Stakes:** The doors are real — if Jake/Blaze misreads a clock, they're trapped until the next cycle (1 hour wait). With limited water in the desert, they can't afford delays.

### Chapter Breakdown

**Chapter 1 — The Entry Chamber**
*Setup:* Jake/Blaze enters the first chamber. Stone clock faces line the walls, each showing different times. A voice echoes: "The Vel'Nara lived by the clock. Only those who know the time may pass."

*Q1 (Warm-Up):* The first clock face: **short hand on 9, long hand on 12. What time is it?** (9:00 — o'clock as warm-up)

**Chapter 2 — The Half-Hour Corridor**
*Rising action:* Jake/Blaze finds Professor Tempo stuck at a door showing half-past 2.

*Q2:* **Clock shows: short hand between 2 and 3, long hand on 6. What time is it?** (2:30 / half past 2). The Professor thought it said 2:06. How many times has he tried? He's been here since 10:30 and it's now 2:30 — **how long has he been stuck?** (4 hours — a duration question embedded naturally)

*Q3:* **Next door shows short hand just past 5, long hand on 3. What time?** (5:15 / quarter past 5)

**Chapter 3 — The Quarter-To Trap**
*Midpoint:* A trap door: it opens at quarter-to-7, but if you read it wrong (as quarter-past-7) it springs early.

*Q4:* **Clock shows short hand approaching 7, long hand on 9. What time is this?** (6:45 / quarter to 7). "Approaching" is the key — the short hand moves toward the hour, not past it.

*Q5:* **Same chamber: another clock shows short hand past 11, long hand on 7. What time?** (11:35 — five-minute intervals)

**Chapter 4 — Race Through the Clock Room**
*Climax:* Jake/Blaze must pass through 5 clock-doors before they all close. Each shows a different time, and the windows only stay open for 5 minutes.

*Q6:* **Clock 1: short hand between 3 and 4, long hand on 4. Time?** (3:20)

*Q7:* **Clock 2: short hand between 6 and 7, long hand on 5. Time?** (6:25)

*Q8:* **Clock 3: short hand between 8 and 9, long hand on 11. Time?** (8:55 — nearly 9 o'clock; the "almost" reading)

**Chapter 5 — The First Chamber Opened**
*Resolution:* All doors cleared. The inner chamber opens.

*Q9:* Inside: a puzzle clock with two hands. It shows 4:40. **The next door opens in 20 minutes. What time will that be?** (5:00 — adding time intervals)

*Q10 (Boss):* Professor Tempo got trapped in the second chamber at 3:15. The door opens every 45 minutes. **Jake/Blaze arrives at 4:35. When does the next door-opening happen after that?** 
- Last opening: 3:15, then 4:00 (45 min later), then 4:45 (45 min after that).
- **Next opening after 4:35 = 4:45** — only 10 minutes away!

**Cliffhanger:** Professor Tempo is free. He immediately says: "The next pyramid — I've been studying it. The doors use 24-hour time! Those are digital clocks!" Jake/Blaze: "You mean the ones you couldn't read earlier?" Tempo: "I can read digital clocks perfectly! It's just those... round ones that give me trouble."

## Issue 2 of 7 — "Elapsed Time in the Echo Hall"

**Title:** The Echo Hall  
**Setting:** The Second Pyramid (Pyramid of Neth) — a hall where sound echoes precisely; doors open based on elapsed time since the last sound  
**Difficulty:** Year 3 — elapsed time, adding time, open number lines

### Story Premise
The Echo Hall works differently: doors don't open at a fixed time — they open after a fixed duration from a trigger sound. Jake/Blaze must calculate when to make a sound (or when the last sound was) to predict when doors will open. The key tool: the open number line.

**Stakes:** Professor Tempo made a sound at the wrong moment and triggered a door that won't reopen for 1 hour 30 minutes. Jake/Blaze must find an alternate route using duration calculations.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Jake/Blaze introduces the open number line to navigate. Q1 (warm-up): A sound at 2:00 opens a door after 25 minutes. **When does the door open?** (2:25 — basic addition, number line optional)

**Ch2:** Duration addition. Q2: Sound at 3:40. Door opens after 35 minutes. **When?** Use number line: 3:40 → 4:00 (20 min) → 4:15 (15 min). **Answer: 4:15**. Q3: Another door: sound at 11:50, door opens after 25 minutes. **Time?** (12:15 — crosses the hour)

**Ch3:** Tempo's mistake. Q4: Tempo made a sound at 1:15. The door he triggered stays shut for 1 hour 30 minutes. **When can it reopen?** (2:45). Q5: It's now 2:20. **How much longer must they wait?** (25 minutes — elapsed time between 2:20 and 2:45)

**Ch4:** Alternate route — 3 doors in sequence. Q6: Door A opens 40 minutes after trigger at 3:00 (opens 3:40). Door B opens 1 hour after A opens: **when?** (4:40). Q7: Door C opens 25 minutes after B: **when?** (5:05). Q8: Jake/Blaze started at 3:00. **Total elapsed time to get through all three doors?** (5:05 − 3:00 = 2 hours 5 minutes — open number line: 3:00→4:00 = 1hr, →5:00 = 1hr, →5:05 = 5min, total = 2h5m)

**Ch5:** Exit the Echo Hall. Q9: Jake/Blaze wants to exit by 5:30. It's now 4:45. **How many minutes until 5:30?** (45 minutes). Q10 (Boss): Jake/Blaze must trigger two sounds: the first at a time so Door X opens at 5:20 (doors open 35 minutes after sound). **When must the first sound be made?** (5:20 − 35 min = 4:45 — reverse elapsed time; work backwards)

**Cliffhanger:** Outside the second pyramid, Tempo has already run ahead to the third pyramid — the one with timetables. He's been there 15 minutes. Inside, a message carved in stone: "The timetable controls all. Miss one step and the schedule collapses."

## Issue 3 of 7 — "The Timetable Tomb"

**Title:** The Schedule of the Ancients  
**Setting:** Third Pyramid (Pyramid of Sevar) — a coordination centre with dozens of moving parts, all controlled by a precise timetable  
**Difficulty:** Year 3–4 — reading and using timetables, duration from timetables, multi-step scheduling

### Story Premise
The Pyramid of Sevar was the Vel'Nara's coordination centre — from here, they scheduled every pyramid event across the civilisation. Its walls show enormous stone timetables. To cross each chamber, Jake/Blaze must read the timetable, calculate arrival times, and synchronise movements with the ancient schedule.

**Stakes:** The timetable controls 4 moving platforms that cycle through the chambers. Step on a platform at the wrong time and it carries you backward. Tempo stepped on Platform 3 at the wrong time and is now one chamber back.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Jake/Blaze studies the stone timetable. Q1 (warm-up): Platform 1 departs at 9:00, arrives at 9:25. **How long is the journey?** (25 minutes)

**Ch2:** Platform schedules. Q2: Platform 2 departs every 20 minutes starting at 10:00. Jake/Blaze arrives at 10:35. **When is the next departure?** (10:40 — 10:00, 10:20, 10:40...). Q3: **How long must they wait?** (5 minutes)

**Ch3:** The timetable has a gap. Q4: Platform 3 schedule: departs 11:15, arrives 11:50. Duration = ? (35 minutes). Tempo needs to catch the NEXT Platform 3 after Tempo's wrong departure at 11:15. **If they depart every hour, when's the next?** (12:15). Q5: **How long does Tempo have to wait?** (From 11:15 to 12:15 = 1 hour)

**Ch4:** Synchronised scheduling problem. Q6: Jake/Blaze needs to be at Chamber 4 by 2:00 PM. The journey through Chamber 3 takes 45 minutes. **What time must they depart Chamber 3?** (1:15 — reverse elapsed time). Q7: Arriving in Chamber 3 takes 30 minutes from Chamber 2. **What time must they depart Chamber 2?** (12:45). Q8: **Total travel time from Chamber 2 to arriving at Chamber 4?** (75 minutes = 1 hour 15 minutes)

**Ch5:** The final timetable coordination. Q9: A stone timetable shows 4 stops with 35-minute intervals starting at 1:00. **List all 4 departure times.** (1:00, 1:35, 2:10, 2:45). Q10 (Boss): Jake/Blaze needs to complete two tasks that can overlap by 15 minutes. Task A takes 50 minutes, Task B takes 40 minutes. Starting at 3:00: **What time do both tasks finish?** (Start both: A = 3:00–3:50, B starts at 3:35 (50−15 = after 35 min), B = 3:35–4:15. Both done at 4:15. Total = 1 hour 15 minutes from 3:00)

**Cliffhanger:** All four platforms reset to starting positions at 5:00 PM. It's now 4:40 PM. Jake/Blaze made it through on time. Tempo made it back on his second try at 4:45 PM. He's proud of himself. "See? I can read a timetable." Jake/Blaze looks at the timetable in Tempo's hand — it's upside down.

## Issue 4 of 7 — "The 24-Hour Chamber"

**Title:** Mission Time  
**Setting:** Fourth Pyramid (Pyramid of Ak) — the military/mission coordination pyramid; runs entirely on 24-hour time  
**Difficulty:** Year 4 — 24-hour time, converting between 12-hour and 24-hour, elapsed time in 24-hour format

### Story Premise
The Pyramid of Ak was the Vel'Nara's military and mission coordination centre. Everything runs on 24-hour time (they called it "mission time"). Professor Tempo has only ever used 12-hour time — he's repeatedly miscalculating, thinking 14:00 is 4 o'clock in the afternoon (subtracting 10 instead of 12). Jake/Blaze must correct him at every turn AND solve the actual time locks.

**Stakes:** There's a hidden chamber that only opens during a specific 30-minute window each day. Miss it and wait 24 hours — which they can't afford.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Introduction to 24-hour time. Q1 (warm-up): **What is 14:00 in 12-hour time?** (2:00 PM). The clock in the entry chamber uses 24-hour format — 24 hours in one complete cycle.

**Ch2:** Reading and converting. Q2: **Convert 9:30 AM to 24-hour time.** (09:30 — same!). Q3: **Convert 21:45 to 12-hour time.** (9:45 PM — subtract 12 for hours after noon).

**Ch3:** Tempo's error. He reads "19:20" as "9:20" (subtracting 10). Q4: **What is 19:20 in 12-hour time?** (7:20 PM — correct Tempo!). Q5: **Elapsed time: 09:30 to 14:15. How long?** (4 hours 45 minutes — open number line: 09:30→10:00=30m, →14:00=4h, →14:15=15m, total 4h45m)

**Ch4:** The hidden chamber window. Q6: The window opens at 16:40 and closes at 17:10. **How long is it open?** (30 minutes). Q7: It's currently 15:55. **How long until it opens?** (45 minutes). Q8: Jake/Blaze needs 20 minutes to reach the chamber from here. **Do they have enough time?** (Yes: they can leave at 16:20 and arrive at 16:40 — exactly on time)

**Ch5:** Through the hidden chamber. Q9: Jake/Blaze enters at 16:40 and must exit before 17:10. The puzzle inside takes 25 minutes. **What time do they exit?** (17:05 — 5 minutes before closing). Q10 (Boss): **The pyramid's daily schedule (24-hr): Opens 06:30. First mission at 08:15. Last mission ends at 22:45. Total open time?** (06:30→22:45 = 16 hours 15 minutes. Last mission start: 22:45 − [duration unknown] — simplify: How many hours from 06:30 to 22:45? Count: 06:30→22:30=16h, +15min=16h15m)

**Cliffhanger:** The hidden chamber has a message: "The fifth pyramid runs on a different calendar. The Vel'Nara counted time in 8-day weeks." Tempo pulls out his notebook and immediately starts converting incorrectly. Jake/Blaze slowly counts to ten.

## Issue 5 of 7 — "The Calendar Pyramid"

**Title:** The 8-Day Week  
**Setting:** Fifth Pyramid (Pyramid of Tar) — records the Vel'Nara's calendar system, different from ours  
**Difficulty:** Year 4 — calendar calculations, converting between units (days/weeks/months), duration in calendar time

### Story Premise
The Vel'Nara calendar has 8-day weeks, 4-week months (32 days), and 10-month years (320 days). Jake/Blaze must convert their own calendar time into Vel'Nara time and back again to solve the pyramid's locks. The entire history of the Vel'Nara is written on the walls in their calendar — and understanding it is the key to the Master Clock.

**Stakes:** The pyramid's inner records reveal EXACTLY when the Master Clock broke — and how to reset it. Without this information, Issue 7's final challenge is impossible.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Learning the Vel'Nara calendar. Q1 (warm-up): **How many days in a Vel'Nara month?** (8 days × 4 weeks = 32 days). **In a year?** (32 × 10 = 320 days)

**Ch2:** Calendar conversions. Q2: **A Vel'Nara record says "Event happened 3 Vel'Nara weeks ago." How many days ago?** (3 × 8 = 24 days). Q3: **"5 Vel'Nara months ago." How many days?** (5 × 32 = 160 days)

**Ch3:** Comparing calendars. Q4: **Our calendar has 7-day weeks. A Vel'Nara week = 8 days. If an event happened 2 of our weeks ago (14 days), how many Vel'Nara weeks is that?** (14 ÷ 8 = 1 week remainder 6 days = 1 week and 6 days). Q5: **The Master Clock broke 10 Vel'Nara years ago. How many Earth days ago?** (10 × 320 = 3,200 days)

**Ch4:** Timeline calculation. Q6: The record says the clock broke on "Day 15 of Month 7 in Year 847." One year = 320 days. **How many days into the year was Day 15 of Month 7?** (6 complete months × 32 days = 192 days + 15 = 207th day of the year). Q7: **If it's currently Day 1 of Month 1 in Year 857 (ten years later), how many Vel'Nara years = how many days?** (10 × 320 = 3,200 days since the break). Q8: **How many Earth years approximately is 3,200 days?** (3,200 ÷ 365 ≈ 8.76 years — approximately 8 years and 9 months)

**Ch5:** The reset code. Q9: The Master Clock must be reset to the moment it broke: Day 207 of Year 847. In the lock code, Day 207 converts to Month and Day: **207 ÷ 32 = 6 months remainder 15 days → Month 7, Day 15.** (207 = 6×32+15; month 7, day 15 ✓). Q10 (Boss): **The clock also needs the exact time (Vel'Nara time). Their day had 20 hours. The clock broke at hour 15. Convert to our 24-hour system. If Vel'Nara Hour 1 = Earth Hour 1.2, what Earth hour is Vel'Nara Hour 15?** (15 × 24/20 = 15 × 1.2 = 18 = 18:00 in 24-hour time = 6:00 PM)

**Cliffhanger:** Jake/Blaze has all the information needed to reset the Master Clock. But when they exit the Pyramid of Tar, Professor Tempo is gone — and there's a note: "I've gone ahead to the Master Clock chamber. I'll fix it myself. — Tempo." Jake/Blaze: "He doesn't have the reset code." The clock chamber is the sixth pyramid. And Tempo is about to break something that's been broken for 3,200 days.

## Issue 6 of 7 — "Race Against Time"

**Title:** Stop the Professor!  
**Setting:** Sixth Pyramid (Pyramid of Vel) — a race against the clock; literally. The whole pyramid is one enormous clock mechanism.  
**Difficulty:** Year 4 — complex elapsed time, multi-step time calculations, time under pressure

### Story Premise
Jake/Blaze races through the Pyramid of Vel to stop Professor Tempo from breaking the Master Clock further. The pyramid itself is a countdown — every 30 minutes, sections of the floor retract into pits. Jake/Blaze must track the time and plan their route around the countdown cycles, while simultaneously doing time-based puzzle locks to open doors.

**Stakes:** The Master Clock. And Tempo's safety — he doesn't realise the floors are on a timer.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Jake/Blaze enters at 14:20. Floor Cycle resets every 30 minutes from 14:00: sections sink at 14:30, 15:00, 15:30... Q1 (warm-up): **First floor section sinks at 14:30. How long does Jake/Blaze have?** (10 minutes)

**Ch2:** Navigating the cycles. Q2: Jake/Blaze needs 8 minutes to cross Section A. They start at 14:22. **Do they make it before the 14:30 sink?** (14:22 + 8 min = 14:30 — exactly! Better run!). Q3: After Section A, they arrive at Section B at 14:32. Next sink at 15:00. Section B takes 22 minutes. **Do they make it?** (14:32 + 22 = 14:54 — yes, 6 minutes before the sink)

**Ch3:** Tempo spotted! He's in Section D, about to tamper with the clock. Section D sinks in 15 minutes. Q4: Jake/Blaze is 2 sections away. Each takes 7 minutes. **Can they reach Tempo before Section D sinks?** (7 + 7 = 14 minutes — 1 minute to spare). Q5: Jake/Blaze warns Tempo. Tempo insists he has time: "I'll be done in 10 minutes." Jake/Blaze: "Section D sinks in 1 minute!" Tempo: "I arrived at 15:10 and it's been about 18 minutes, so it's 15:28." **Is Tempo right?** (15:10 + 18 min = 15:28 ✓ — he's right about the time but wrong about the sink cycle: sinks at 15:30 NOT 15:00 — check: 14:00, 14:30, 15:00, 15:30. Next sink = 15:30, not 15:28. **How long until 15:30?** (2 minutes))

**Ch4:** Reaching the Master Clock chamber. Q6: It's 15:45. The chamber lock requires entering today's date in Vel'Nara time (from Issue 5). **The date Jake/Blaze calculated: Year 857, Month 1, Day 1. Entry code: 857-1-1. How many Vel'Nara days since Year 1, Month 1, Day 1?** (857 × 320 = simplify: 857 × 320 = 274,240 days. Too complex for Year 3/4 — adjust: Q6 is "The lock needs the year in 24-hour time format. Convert 18:00 to a number the lock recognises: 18. What was the hour the clock broke?" (18:00 → 18). Q7: The next lock: elapsed time from 14:20 (entry) to now (15:45). **How long have they been inside?** (1 hour 25 minutes). Q8: They need to exit by 17:30. **How much time do they have left?** (17:30 − 15:45 = 1 hour 45 minutes)

**Ch5:** Jake/Blaze reaches the Master Clock. Q9: The clock's reset mechanism asks for elapsed time since it broke: 3,200 days. **Convert to years and leftover days in OUR calendar: 3,200 ÷ 365 = ?** (8 years remainder 280 days). Q10 (Boss): The reset code requires the EXACT time in 24-hour format when the clock broke (18:00) AND the total minutes since a reference point. Vel'Nara Day 207 of Year 847 × 20 hours per day = **207 × 20 = 4,140 Vel'Nara hours since the year started. Convert to minutes: 4,140 × 60 = 248,400 minutes.** (The code is 248400.) The final lock clicks open.

**Cliffhanger:** The Master Clock is right in front of them. But it's even more complex than expected — it has THREE hands, not two. And it's pointing at times that don't make sense. Tempo looks at it and finally says: "I don't know what time this says. I need your help."

## Issue 7 of 7 — "Fixing the Master Clock"

**Title:** The Time Before Time  
**Setting:** The Heart of the Seventh Pyramid — the Master Clock Chamber  
**Difficulty:** Year 4 — multi-step time, connections across all time concepts, the 3-handed clock

### Story Premise
The Master Clock has three hands: hours, minutes, and a third hand that shows the "Vel'Nara meridian" — a concept that splits their day into 4 equal quarters of 5 hours each. To reset it, Jake/Blaze must set all three hands correctly using every time skill learned throughout the arc. Tempo genuinely helps in the final act — his knowledge of the Vel'Nara calendar complements Jake/Blaze's time-calculation skills.

**Stakes:** Resetting the Master Clock. The entire Vel'Nara civilisation. And whether Professor Tempo ever learns to read a clock properly.

### Chapter Breakdown

**Ch1:** Understanding the three-handed clock. *Q1:* In Vel'Nara time, the day has 20 hours. The third hand divides the day into 4 quarters. **How many hours per quarter?** (20 ÷ 4 = 5 hours per quarter). Vel'Nara Quarter 1 = Hours 1–5, Quarter 2 = Hours 6–10, etc.

**Ch2:** Setting the hours hand. *Q2:* The clock broke at Hour 15 of the Vel'Nara day. **Which quarter is Hour 15 in?** (Hours 11–15 = Quarter 3 — count: Q1=1-5, Q2=6-10, Q3=11-15, Q4=16-20). *Q3:* Translate: Earth time equivalent of Vel'Nara Hour 15 = 18:00. **What is 18:00 in 12-hour AM/PM time?** (6:00 PM)

**Ch3:** Setting the minutes hand. The Vel'Nara minute is slightly longer than ours — 1 Vel'Nara minute = 1.2 Earth minutes. *Q4:* The clock broke at Vel'Nara Minute 30 of Hour 15. **Convert 30 Vel'Nara minutes to Earth minutes.** (30 × 1.2 = 36 Earth minutes → simplify for Year 3/4: state as given fact, Q4 = "The clock broke at 18:00 and 36 minutes. Write this as a 24-hour time." (18:36)). *Q5:* **How many minutes is it from 18:36 until 19:00?** (24 minutes — elapsed time for the "minutes hand correction")

**Ch4:** The third hand — the Meridian. *Q6:* The Meridian hand shows which quarter of the day it is. Quarter 3 spans Hours 11–15. **How long is Quarter 3 in Earth time?** (5 Vel'Nara hours × 1.2 = 6 Earth hours). *Q7:* The Meridian hand must point to the exact fraction of Quarter 3 when the clock broke. Hour 15 is the LAST hour of Q3. **What fraction through Q3 is Hour 15?** (5/5 = the very end — so the hand points at the end of the quarter arc). *Q8:* **If Quarter 1 starts at Earth time 06:00, when does Quarter 3 end?** (Q1: 06:00–12:00, Q2: 12:00–18:00, Q3: 18:00–24:00 — end = midnight / 00:00)

**Ch5 — Resolution:** All three hands set. Jake/Blaze and Tempo insert the reset code together. The Master Clock begins to tick — for the first time in 3,200 days. Around them, the pyramid lights up. In the chamber walls, carvings begin to glow with moving scenes: the Vel'Nara living, building, calculating.

*Q9:* The clock is running! It shows 18:36. It's currently 22:15 in Earth time. **How much time has passed since the clock broke?** (22:15 − 18:36 = 3 hours 39 minutes — using the number line: 18:36 → 19:00 = 24min, → 22:00 = 3hr, → 22:15 = 15min. Total = 3h 39min)

*Q10 (Boss):* The Vel'Nara civilisation stirs — their descendants, scattered across the planet, all receive a signal. They were each told to return "when the clock runs again, at exactly 3 hours after the reset." The clock reset at 18:36. **What time do they return?** (18:36 + 3 hours = 21:36 in 24-hour time = 9:36 PM). It is currently 22:15. **Did they arrive on time or have they already passed?** (21:36 < 22:15 — they've already come and gone! Jake/Blaze missed them by 39 minutes!)

**Resolution:** The descendants came — and left a message, because they knew someone would come. The message describes where they have rebuilt their civilisation, and invites Jake/Blaze to visit. It's signed by the current Vel'Nara leader: "Time waits for no one. But we waited for you."

Professor Tempo finally reads the message clock correctly: "They left at... 21:36." He pauses. "I read it right." Jake/Blaze smiles. "You did, Tempo."

## Narrative Tone Notes

**Emotional arc for the reader:**  
Issues 1–2: wonder and focus (clocks are magical, time is everywhere — urgency from the very first chapter)  
Issues 3–4: growing mastery and real-world connection (timetables = daily life; 24-hour = adventure)  
Issues 5–6: complexity and tension (races against time, a race to save someone)  
Issue 7: resolution and bittersweet beauty (the Vel'Nara restored, but just missed — time doesn't stand still for anyone)

**What makes this world feel distinct:**  
The pyramids have an ancient, dusty, reverent feel — very different from the living jungle or the stars of space. Time is the civilisation's religion; it's everywhere in the architecture. The traps and locks being time-based creates genuine urgency. Professor Tempo is a loveable fool — his inability to tell the time correctly is played for warm comedy but also for genuine learning (every time he gets it wrong, readers see WHY it matters). The bittersweet ending (the Vel'Nara were missed by 39 minutes!) makes time feel real and urgent and irreversible.

**Key recurring elements:**  
- Professor Tempo (loveable antagonist; grows across all 7 issues; has his big moment in Issue 7)
- The three-handed clock (introduced in Issue 7 but foreshadowed by "three clues" across the arc)
- The open number line (explicitly used in every elapsed time problem — a consistent visual tool)
- The 30-minute floor-sink cycle in Issue 6 (creates genuine tension through math)
- The countdown framing — "you must be there by X time" — throughout the arc
