# CHARACTER PROFILE — TANGLE
## *Math Blast* Jungle Arc — "The Vanishing Village"

---

## OVERVIEW

**Name:** Tangle  
**Role:** Arc antagonist (Issues 1–5) → Arc ally (Issues 6–7)  
**Nature:** A large, ancient, plant-like creature native to the deep Verdant Canopy jungle  
**Appearance:** A walking tumbleweed of colourful vines — enormous, slow-moving, gentle. Vines trail and shift constantly. Has five toes (the footprint Blaze finds in Issue 1). No visible face in the conventional sense — "expression" is communicated through the arrangement of vines.  
**Intelligence:** High, but non-verbal and culturally isolated. Has lived alone in the Deep Root for centuries making mathematical vine sculptures. Has never interacted with the Sprockets or other beings before this arc.

---

## PERSONALITY

Tangle is **not a villain.** This is the most important thing.

Tangle collected vines because they are beautiful to it. It didn't know the vines connected the villages. It has no concept of community, sharing, or consequence — because it has never been part of one. It has lived alone so long that it has invented its own private mathematics without knowing it.

**Core traits:**
- **Curious** — watches from the shadows long before approaching
- **Gentle** — never aggressive, always cautious when discovered
- **Precise** — everything it does is deliberate and patterned. Its vine bundles are always perfect. Its sculptures are flawless arrays.
- **Confused by remainders** — this is its key emotional vulnerability. Tangle finds the concept of "left over" deeply unsettling. Things that don't fit into equal groups distress it.
- **Fascinated by sharing** — once it understands what the Sprockets do, it wants to learn it. It has never had anyone to share with.

**The number 12:** Tangle's favourite number. All of its sculptures are built around multiples of 12. When Blaze notices this pattern (Issue 4), it's a turning point — she's understanding Tangle's language.

---

## COMMUNICATION

Tangle **does not speak human language.** It communicates through:
1. **Vine arrangements** — dropping bundles at someone's feet as greeting/offering
2. **Body posture** — crouching vs standing, vine-trailing vs vine-coiling
3. **Patterns** — arranging objects in recognisable mathematical shapes (arrays, groups)

**From Issue 6 onwards**, when Blaze tries to teach Tangle about sharing, Tangle begins to "speak" through the narrator's interpretation of its vine-patterns — translated for the reader. This is never dialogue in the traditional sense; it's always mediated by Eric's narration.

**Audio approach:**
- **Issues 1–5:** No Tangle voice. Eric (narrator) describes Tangle's presence and movements.
- **Issue 6 (reveal):** A single distinctive sound cue — a deep, resonant hum — when Tangle first makes full contact with Blaze. This signals a change in their relationship.
- **Issue 7 (alliance):** Tangle's "responses" are narrated by Eric as vine-pattern translations.

**Tangle's audio signature (for Issue 6+):**  
- **Voice ID:** N2lVS1w4EtoT3dr4eOWO (Callum — deep, Scottish, distinctive)
- **Model:** eleven_multilingual_v2
- **Settings:** stability=0.85, similarity_boost=0.30, style=0.55
- **Rationale:** Deep, earthy, not quite human. High stability for slowness and deliberateness. Low similarity for otherworldliness. Medium style for warmth — Tangle must feel safe, not scary.
- **Usage:** Minimal. 1–3 very short utterances per issue maximum. The rarity is the point.

## WHAT TANGLE TEACHES

Tangle is a mathematical creature who doesn't know it's mathematical. Its love of equal groups, arrays, and patterns is instinctive — not learned. When Blaze helps it understand what remainders mean (Issue 5), and what sharing means (Issue 6), Tangle doesn't just learn maths. It learns belonging.

The emotional resolution of the arc: Tangle builds a sculpture in a clearing (not on a vine-path) as its first gift to a community it's now part of. Equal groups. Perfect arrays. The same mathematics — but now in service of others, not just itself.

**For kids:** Tangle shows that being good at patterns doesn't make you good at sharing — and that the best thing to do with a gift is use it for others.

---

## WRITER'S NOTES

- Never make Tangle threatening after Issue 3. Mysterious, yes. Looming, yes. But the feeling should shift from "danger" to "misunderstood" by Issue 4.
- Tangle's confusion about remainders in Issue 5 is the emotional centrepiece of the whole arc. Handle it with care — it's the moment the reader's sympathy fully transfers.
- Tangle is never referred to with a gender pronoun. "It" throughout. This is intentional.
- The number 12 is always Tangle's tell. If Blaze finds vine sculptures with 12, 24, 36, 48, 60, 72... Tangle has been there.
