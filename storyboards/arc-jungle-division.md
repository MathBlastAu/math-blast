# Arc Storyboard: Jungle — Division
## "The Vanishing Village"

**World:** The Verdant Canopy — a vast, ancient jungle on a distant planet, home to the Sprocket tribe (small, clever, tree-dwelling aliens) and many hidden creatures  
**Math Topic:** Division (partitive and quotitive; remainders; connection to fractions)  
**ACARA Content:** AC9M3A02, AC9M3A03 → AC9M4A02 (Year 3 → Year 4)  
**Issues:** 7  
**Character Choice:** Jake (methodical, thorough) or Blaze (instinctive, fast-moving)

---

## Arc Overview

### The Big Story
The Sprocket tribe has lived in the Verdant Canopy for 10,000 years. Their whole culture runs on **equal sharing** — every berry, every shelter, every tool is divided perfectly among tribe members. It's the foundation of their civilization. But something has gone wrong: tribe members are disappearing from their villages, one by one, and the ones who remain are running out of resources — because their division is based on the old total number of people.

Jake/Blaze arrives as a "canopy explorer" and discovers that the disappearances are connected to the **Vine Web** — an ancient natural network of vines that the Sprockets use to travel the jungle. Someone (or something) has been cutting specific vines, isolating parts of the tribe. With each village cut off, the remaining group keeps dividing the same resources by the wrong number.

The mystery creature responsible turns out to be **Tangle** — a confused jungle creature who doesn't understand sharing. It's been collecting vines (which it finds beautiful) without realising it's cutting off the Sprockets. The resolution is teaching Tangle what equal sharing means.

### Villain/Antagonist  
**Tangle** — a large, gentle, plant-like creature that looks like a walking tumbleweed of colourful vines. It doesn't speak, but communicates through the patterns it makes with vines. Not evil — just doesn't understand consequences. Issue by issue, Jake/Blaze spots signs of Tangle's activity and gets closer to understanding what it is.

### How Division Threads Through the Arc
- **Issues 1–2:** Partitive division (sharing equally) — the Sprockets' core cultural practice
- **Issues 3–4:** Quotitive division (grouping/measurement) — "how many groups can we make?"
- **Issues 5–6:** Remainders and their meaning — what happens when things don't divide evenly?
- **Issue 7:** Division connecting to fractions (3 ÷ 4 = ¾) — the elegant resolution

### Jake vs Blaze Personality Differences
| Aspect | Jake | Blaze |
|--------|------|-------|
| Approach | Draws arrays and tables; likes to write out all the steps | Physically sorts objects; "let me just try it" |
| Relationship with Sprockets | Asks careful questions, takes notes | Sits with them, watches what they do, participates |
| Approach to Tangle | Cautious, observes from a distance first | Reaches out immediately when she realises Tangle isn't dangerous |
| Voice | "If there are 24 berries and 4 Sprockets..." | "Okay so everyone gets the same — I'll just count it out." |

## Issue 1 of 7 — "The Sprocket Village"

**Title:** Welcome to the Canopy  
**Setting:** Treetop Village of Branchwick — the first Sprocket settlement, high in the jungle canopy  
**Difficulty:** Year 3 entry — partitive division, small numbers, fact families

### Story Premise
Jake/Blaze crash-lands (or crash-vines) into the canopy and is greeted by Chief Pip of Branchwick. The Sprockets are preparing the evening meal — but their chief berry-counter, Doz, has wandered off and hasn't returned. Someone needs to divide the day's harvest or no one eats tonight.

**Stakes:** If the food isn't divided fairly and exactly, Sprocket custom says no one may eat until it's fixed. The whole village goes to bed hungry.

### Chapter Breakdown

**Chapter 1 — Crash into the Canopy**
*Setup:* Jake/Blaze descends into Branchwick after a vine-break sends them swinging wildly through the trees. Chief Pip (tiny, bright blue, three antennae) explains the problem.

*Q1 (Warm-Up):* Chief Pip's greeting ritual involves sharing 12 welcome-berries equally among the 3 visitors who arrived last week. **How many berries per visitor?** (12 ÷ 3 = 4) — basic partitive division, physically demonstrated by the Sprockets.

**Chapter 2 — The Berry Harvest**
*Rising action:* Jake/Blaze finds the harvest basket. Tonight's haul: 24 glow-berries for 4 Sprocket families.

*Q2:* 24 glow-berries ÷ 4 families = ? Each family gets how many? (6). **Draw an array or sharing diagram.**

*Q3:* A second basket has 18 moon-figs for 6 families. 18 ÷ 6 = ? (3 each). **What multiplication fact proves your answer?** (6 × 3 = 18 — fact family)

**Chapter 3 — The Missing Doz**
*Midpoint:* Doz isn't lost — they're found in the next tree over, staring at a strange tangle of vines that wasn't there yesterday. Massive, colourful, completely blocking the path between villages.

*Q4:* The Sprocket medic has 20 healing leaves. She wants to sort them into groups of 5 to make pouches. **How many pouches can she make?** (20 ÷ 5 = 4 — quotitive division introduced, first time grouping vs sharing is explicit)

*Q5:* Same medic, 15 leaves, groups of 3. **How many groups?** (15 ÷ 3 = 5). **Write the full fact family.** (3 × 5 = 15, 5 × 3 = 15, 15 ÷ 3 = 5, 15 ÷ 5 = 3)

**Chapter 4 — The Feast Begins**
*Climax:* The feast is prepped. Now Jake/Blaze must solve the biggest division problems to get the cooking started.

*Q6:* The cook has 36 fire-nuts for 9 Sprockets. **36 ÷ 9 = ?** (4 each)

*Q7:* 32 pieces of bark-bread for 8 Sprockets. **32 ÷ 8 = ?** (4 each). **Notice anything?** (Same answer — connect to pattern recognition)

*Q8:* Chief Pip wants to know: if there had been 5 families instead of 4, how many berries each from the original 24? **24 ÷ 5 = ?** (4 remainder 4 — first remainder! What do the Sprockets do with the leftover?)

**Chapter 5 — Tangle's First Footprint**
*Resolution:* Feast is shared. But Jake/Blaze goes back to look at the mysterious vine tangle — and in the mud nearby, they spot the most enormous footprint they've ever seen.

*Q9:* Jake/Blaze counts the vine clusters removed: 28 vines in 7 neat bundles. **28 ÷ 7 = ?** (4 per bundle — the footprints match something that gathers in groups of 4)

*Q10 (Boss):* Chief Pip wants to plan tomorrow's meal early. The village has 45 food items for 6 families. **45 ÷ 6 = ? What happens to the remainder?** (7 remainder 3 — the 3 extras go to the medic as emergency rations — real-world remainder reasoning)

**Cliffhanger:** That night, more vines go missing from the path to the neighbouring village of Fernmoss. One family from Branchwick who went to visit Fernmoss... hasn't come back.

## Issue 2 of 7 — "The Fernmoss Muddle"

**Title:** Dividing in the Dark  
**Setting:** Fernmoss — a lower-canopy village built around glowing mushrooms; partially cut off from Branchwick  
**Difficulty:** Year 3 — partitive division, fact families, larger numbers

### Story Premise
Jake/Blaze crosses to Fernmoss using an alternate route. The Fernmoss Sprockets are still dividing their resources as if all their members are present — but 8 members got cut off when Tangle took the connecting vines. The division is wrong; some Sprockets are getting too little.

**Stakes:** Fernmoss's medicine store is being divided wrong — a sick Sprocket isn't getting enough medicine.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Jake/Blaze arrives at Fernmoss. The medicine is being distributed 36 doses ÷ 12 Sprockets. But only 4 are present. Q1 (warm-up): **36 ÷ 12 = ?** (3 each for 12). With only 4 present: **36 ÷ 4 = ?** (9 each — much more, they've been underdosing!)

**Ch2:** Food distribution re-calculation. Q2: 48 nuts for however many Sprockets are actually present (8). **48 ÷ 8 = ?** (6 each). Q3: The sick Sprocket needs 4 times a normal dose. Normal dose = 9. **9 × 4 = ?** — uses the multiplication-division link.

**Ch3:** Jake/Blaze helps count all remaining items. Q4: 56 leaves ÷ 7 families = ? (8 each). Q5: A storage box has 63 seeds. How many groups of 9 can be made? (63 ÷ 9 = 7 — quotitive)

**Ch4:** A pattern emerges in Tangle's vine removal — it's systematically taking vines that connect specific villages. Q6: 42 vines missing. If Tangle takes 6 vines at a time, how many trips did it make? (42 ÷ 6 = 7). Q7: Jake/Blaze spots fresh vine tangles — 35 new ones in 5 bundles. How many per bundle? (7). Q8: Tangle seems to travel 8 km in a day. If the distance from here to the next village is 72 km of vine-trail, how many days until Tangle reaches it? (72 ÷ 8 = 9 — rate/distance division)

**Ch5:** Jake/Blaze fixes Fernmoss's distribution system. Q9: Total Fernmoss resources: 81 items for 9 Sprockets. **81 ÷ 9 = ?** (9 each). Q10 (Boss): The sick Sprocket needs 3/4 of their share in the first day. **3/4 of 9 = ?** (6¾ — introduces the fraction connection to division as 3 × 9 ÷ 4 = 6.75 ≈ 6¾)

**Cliffhanger:** Jake/Blaze finds a trail of enormous pressed-vine footprints leading toward the ancient heart of the jungle — toward the Deep Root, the most ancient part of the canopy.

## Issue 3 of 7 — "Grouping at the Waterfall"

**Title:** How Many Groups?  
**Setting:** Cascade Clearing — a misty waterfall clearing in the middle-canopy; home to the most skilled division-problem solvers in the jungle, the Splash-Sprockets  
**Difficulty:** Year 3–4 — quotitive division (grouping), connecting to repeated subtraction

### Story Premise
The Splash-Sprockets don't share into equal groups — they make groups of a set size. Their whole way of life is "how many groups of X can we make?" rather than "how do we share among N people?" Jake/Blaze must learn their way of division to solve problems in this new community — and discovers that quotitive thinking reveals something new about Tangle.

**Stakes:** The Splash-Sprockets are preparing for a festival that only happens every 100 years. Everything must be organised in groups of specific sizes. If the grouping is wrong, the festival fails and tradition is broken.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Festival prep begins. 60 floating lanterns need to be arranged in groups of 10. Q1 (warm-up): **60 ÷ 10 = ?** (6 groups). Jake/Blaze sets up the lanterns — they can see the 6 groups physically.

**Ch2:** More festival items. Q2: 45 ribbon-flowers ÷ groups of 9 = ? (5 groups). Q3: 72 sparkle-stones ÷ groups of 8 = ? (9 groups). These are visual, physical groupings — the reader imagines arranging them.

**Ch3:** A clue about Tangle. Vine measurements: Tangle has collected 84 vines. It always bundles them in groups of 12. Q4: **84 ÷ 12 = ?** (7 bundles). Q5: Jake/Blaze finds a cave with 96 vines. If Tangle makes groups of 6: **96 ÷ 6 = ?** (16 groups). The pattern: Tangle is organising, not destroying.

**Ch4:** Festival challenge — the Splash-Sprocket elder sets Jake/Blaze a grouping competition. Q6: 100 items, groups of 4 — how many groups? (25). Q7: Same 100 items, groups of 5 — how many groups? (20). **Which makes more groups — groups of 4 or groups of 5?** (Groups of 4 — smaller group size = more groups)

*Q8:* 57 berries, groups of 8. **57 ÷ 8 = ?** (7 groups remainder 1 — the remaining berry becomes a "half-berry" ceremony at the festival; remainders have cultural meaning)

**Ch5:** Jake/Blaze wins the festival challenge. Q9: 156 ÷ 12 = ? (13 groups). Q10 (Boss): The elder's final test: 200 sparkle-stones, groups of... the reader must choose the group size to make exactly 25 groups. **200 ÷ ? = 25. What is the divisor?** (200 ÷ 25 = 8 — working backwards from the quotient to find the divisor)

**Cliffhanger:** The festival begins — and through the jungle, a glimpse: Tangle, watching from the shadows between the trees. But it doesn't look threatening. It's mimicking the grouping patterns of the festival lanterns with its own vine bundles.

## Issue 4 of 7 — "The Array Forest"

**Title:** Rows and Columns in the Canopy  
**Setting:** The Array Forest — a mathematically perfect section of jungle where trees grow in exact rows and columns (it's a Sprocket heritage site)  
**Difficulty:** Year 4 — division from arrays, 2-digit division, area model for division

### Story Premise
The Array Forest is the Sprockets' most sacred site — trees planted in perfect rectangular arrays by ancient mathematicians. But Tangle has moved through the forest, disturbing some of the rows. If Jake/Blaze can't restore the arrays and count what's in each one, the Sprocket historians can't verify their records — and the tribe's history is erased.

**Stakes:** The historical records. Also: Jake/Blaze gets close enough to observe Tangle directly — it's clearly making its own array patterns with vine bundles.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** The first disturbed array. Records say it was 24 trees in 4 equal rows. Q1 (warm-up): **24 ÷ 4 = ?** (6 trees per row). Jake/Blaze helps replant them into the right array.

**Ch2:** Second array. Records say 35 trees in 5 rows. Q2: **35 ÷ 5 = ?** (7 per row). Third array: 48 trees in 6 rows. Q3: **48 ÷ 6 = ?** (8 per row). Building fluency.

**Ch3:** Larger arrays — these are the prestige plantings. Q4: 96 trees in 8 rows. **96 ÷ 8 = ?** (12 per row). Use an area model to solve. Q5: 84 trees in 7 rows. **84 ÷ 7 = ?** (12 per row). Area model again.

**Ch4:** Tangle's array. Jake/Blaze finds that Tangle has arranged 132 vines into 11 neat bundles. Q6: **132 ÷ 11 = ?** (12 per bundle — Tangle LOVES groups of 12!). Q7: Tangle's vine pattern forms a rectangle: 12 wide, ? rows. There are 72 vines. **72 ÷ 12 = ?** (6 rows). Q8: If Tangle makes one more row of 12, total vines = ? **(72 + 12 = 84; 84 ÷ 12 = 7 rows)**

**Ch5:** The historical records are restored. Jake/Blaze pieces together Tangle's behaviour: it loves making perfect arrays. Q9: **156 ÷ 12 = ?** (13). Q10 (Boss): Tangle has a collection of 168 vines. It makes arrays with exactly 12 in each row. **168 ÷ 12 = how many rows?** (14 rows). And the area of that vine array? **(14 × 12 = 168 square vine-units)**

**Cliffhanger:** Jake/Blaze reaches out a hand toward Tangle. It doesn't run. It drops a bundle of 12 vines at Jake/Blaze's feet — a gift? An invitation?

## Issue 5 of 7 — "Remainders and Rescues"

**Title:** What to Do with the Leftovers?  
**Setting:** Split Ridge — a cliff-face village where the terrain means resources are always split unevenly  
**Difficulty:** Year 4 — remainders, what remainders mean in context, rounding up vs rounding down

### Story Premise
Split Ridge is the village with the worst remainder problem in the jungle. Nothing divides evenly there — the terrain makes groups irregular and leftovers constant. The villagers have different opinions about what to do with remainders: some round up, some throw them away, some argue about who gets the extra. Jake/Blaze must mediate and solve real remainder problems where the answer depends on context.

**Stakes:** A group of Sprockets is stranded on the far side of a ridge. They need exactly the right number of rope bridges to get across — and the numbers don't divide neatly.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Jake/Blaze arrives. Q1 (warm-up): 13 Sprockets need to cross using rafts that hold 4. **13 ÷ 4 = 3 remainder 1.** How many rafts needed? (4 — round UP to save everyone)

**Ch2:** A different remainder problem. Q2: 22 rocks for 5 Sprockets — equal shares, remainder expressed as a fraction: **22 ÷ 5 = 4 remainder 2 = 4 and 2/5 each.** Q3: 19 fire-nuts for 6 Sprockets. **19 ÷ 6 = 3 remainder 1.** What do they do? Context: the remainder goes to the eldest — discuss as a tradition.

**Ch3:** Tangle is spotted here. It's brought 50 vines to the ridge. Q4: Tangle arranges them in groups of 8. **50 ÷ 8 = 6 remainder 2.** Tangle is confused — it can see the 2 leftover vines. It doesn't know what to do! (Key moment — Tangle experiences what a remainder feels like.) Q5: Jake/Blaze shows Tangle: if you make groups of 5 instead: **50 ÷ 5 = 10 groups, no remainder.** Tangle seems happier.

**Ch4:** The bridge problem. Q6: 27 planks available. Each bridge needs 6. **27 ÷ 6 = 4 remainder 3.** Do they have enough for 4 bridges plus a partial 5th? (Yes — 4 complete, 3 planks left over). Q7: 4 bridges for 31 Sprockets. **31 ÷ 4 = 7 remainder 3.** How many per bridge, and which bridge carries 1 extra? Q8: Total travel time at 8 Sprockets per minute crossing: **31 Sprockets ÷ 8 per minute = 3 remainder 7 → 4 minutes needed**

**Ch5:** Rescue complete. Q9: The rescued Sprockets bring a gift: 43 star-fruits for the whole village of 7 families. **43 ÷ 7 = 6 remainder 1.** Express the remainder as a fraction: **6 and 1/7 per family.** Q10 (Boss): The village needs to send equal amounts to three other cut-off villages. They have 100 units of food. **100 ÷ 3 = 33 remainder 1.** Two villages get 33, one gets 34 — but is that still "equal enough"? Discuss. What fraction of the total does each village get approximately? **(≈ 1/3)**

**Cliffhanger:** Tangle has followed Jake/Blaze from Split Ridge. It's sitting just outside the village, clearly watching. And it's brought all the vines it took — it seems to want to give them back.

## Issue 6 of 7 — "Tangle's Mistake"

**Title:** The Creature Who Didn't Know  
**Setting:** The Deep Root — the ancient centre of the canopy; Tangle's home territory  
**Difficulty:** Year 4 — short division, dividing 3-digit by 1-digit, division as the inverse of multiplication

### Story Premise
Jake/Blaze follows Tangle to the Deep Root and discovers the truth: Tangle didn't know the vines were connecting villages. It was building its own array patterns — an art form. It's been making intricate vine sculptures for centuries, completely alone. The vine sculptures ARE beautiful. But they've been causing havoc. Now Jake/Blaze must help Tangle understand what sharing and dividing means, so it can find a way to practice its art without cutting off the villages.

**Stakes:** All the missing Sprockets are at the Deep Root — they followed a path of beautiful vine sculptures deeper and deeper into the jungle until they were lost. They need to be brought home, but the path divides confusingly.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Jake/Blaze arrives at the Deep Root. Tangle's vine sculptures are extraordinary — huge, mathematical, arrays and patterns everywhere. Q1 (warm-up): The largest sculpture is a rectangle of 144 vines in 12 rows. **144 ÷ 12 = ?** (12 per row — a perfect square array!)

**Ch2:** Finding the missing Sprockets. They're scattered across 5 groups. Q2: 60 Sprockets total, 5 groups — equal groups for safe travel back. **60 ÷ 5 = ?** (12 each). Q3: One group moved further in — 84 Sprockets divided into groups of 7 for the narrow vine-bridge. **84 ÷ 7 = ?** (12 per group — again 12! Tangle's favourite number)

**Ch3:** Jake/Blaze explains sharing to Tangle using vine sculptures. Q4: Tangle has 156 short vines. It wants to share them equally with the Sprockets so they can fix their vine bridges. **156 ÷ 12 = ?** (13 — 13 bundles for 13 villages!). Q5: Each village gets one bundle of 12. But there are 15 villages. **15 × 12 = 180. Tangle needs 180 − 156 = 24 more vines.** How many more bundles is that? **(24 ÷ 12 = 2 more bundles)**

**Ch4:** Short division with Tangle's vine collections. Q6: Tangle has 252 vines. **252 ÷ 6 = ?** (42 — use short division method). Q7: **252 ÷ 7 = ?** (36 — short division again). Q8: Which is bigger — 42 or 36? (42, from ÷6). So dividing by a smaller number gives a bigger result. **Why?** (Each group is smaller, so more groups fit — inverse relationship explained)

**Ch5:** Tangle promises to build its sculptures only in clearings, never on vine-paths. Q9: The clearing is 13 metres wide, 9 metres long. **Area = 13 × 9 = 117 square metres.** How many 9-metre rows can Tangle fit? **(117 ÷ 9 = 13 rows)**. Q10 (Boss): Tangle has 288 vines for its new clearing sculpture. It wants rows of 12. **288 ÷ 12 = ?** Using short division: 28÷12=2 r4, bring down 8, 48÷12=4. **Answer: 24 rows**

**Cliffhanger:** Tangle leads Jake/Blaze to the very centre of the Deep Root — and shows them something astonishing: a vine sculpture so large it can be seen from above the canopy. It's a pattern that looks like... an enormous number line. Tangle has been making math art its whole life without knowing it.

## Issue 7 of 7 — "The Great Sharing"

**Title:** The Day Everything Was Shared Fairly  
**Setting:** All five villages — the whole Verdant Canopy comes together for the first time in centuries  
**Difficulty:** Year 4 — division with larger numbers, division connecting to fractions, multi-step division problems

### Story Premise
Tangle guides Jake/Blaze and all the missing Sprockets home. The villages — separated for so long — reunite. Now all their resources need to be redistributed fairly across the whole canopy community. It's the biggest division problem anyone has ever faced. Tangle helps — it turns out to be an extraordinary organiser, its love of equal groups finally put to good use.

**Stakes:** The whole tribe's survival through the coming Dry Season depends on the resources being fairly shared. Wrong division means some villages starve.

### Chapter Breakdown

**Ch1:** All five villages gathered. Total community count: 120 Sprockets.
*Q1:* Chief Pip asks: if all food is shared equally, each of 120 Sprockets gets how much from a store of 360 items? **360 ÷ 120 = ?** (3 each)

**Ch2:** Village-by-village allocation. Q2: Village Fernmoss (24 Sprockets) should get their portion of 360 items. **24/120 = 1/5. 360 × 1/5 = ?** (72 items). Q3: Village Split Ridge (30 Sprockets) should get: **30/120 = 1/4. 360 × 1/4 = ?** (90 items)

**Ch3:** Tangle's vine-bridge rebuild plan. Q4: 5 broken bridges, each needs 48 vines. Total vines: **5 × 48 = 240.** Tangle has 240. **240 ÷ 48 = 5 — perfect, one bundle per bridge.** Q5: Workers need to build 3 bridges per day. **5 ÷ 3 = 1 remainder 2.** How many full days for the first 3, and how many bridges on day 2? (Day 1: 3. Day 2: 2 — remainder bridges)

**Ch4:** Dry Season prep — the biggest division. Q6: 504 water pods for 7 villages. **504 ÷ 7 = ?** (72 per village). Q7: Each village needs to last 4 months. **72 ÷ 4 = 18 pods per month.** Q8: Branchwick has 5 families. **18 ÷ 5 = 3 remainder 3.** Express as a mixed number: **3 and 3/5 pods per family per month.** Tangle demonstrates: 3 whole pods + 3/5 of one more, divided into 5 pieces.

**Ch5 — Resolution:**
Q9: Final distribution check. Total items distributed: 72 + 90 + 54 + 63 + 81 = 360. Verify: **360 ÷ 5 villages (if equal) = 72** — but they weren't equal (different population sizes). **Was it fair?** (Yes — proportional to population). Ask: what does "fair" really mean?

Q10 (Boss): Tangle's final mathematical act: it arranges the whole village gathering into a perfect array. **480 Sprockets (all villages together) in rows of 16. 480 ÷ 16 = ?** (30 rows). And in the shape of a rectangle on the jungle floor... it looks exactly like a multiplication array. Tangle understands at last.

**Resolution:** The Dry Season is prepared for. All villages are connected by new vine bridges — and Tangle has a new role: official Canopy Bridge Builder. It builds sculptures in clearings AND functional vine bridges in equal-row patterns. Chief Pip declares it an Honorary Sprocket.

Jake/Blaze returns home with a bundle of vine-art from Tangle — the most mathematically perfect present they've ever received.

## Narrative Tone Notes

**Emotional arc for the reader:**  
Issues 1–2: warmth and welcome (the Sprockets are charming, the sharing is joyful)  
Issues 3–4: growing competence (larger numbers, the world getting bigger)  
Issues 5–6: genuine moral complexity (what IS fair when things don't divide evenly? Tangle revealed as sympathetic)  
Issue 7: resolution and joy (the whole community together; math as the foundation of fairness)

**What makes this world feel distinct:**  
The jungle is alive, tactile, and physical. Division is literally about sharing food, building bridges, fitting through narrow paths. Unlike space (clean, digital) or pyramids (historical, dramatic), the jungle is warm, funny, and culturally rich. The Sprockets' civilisation runs entirely on equal sharing — division isn't just math, it's their way of life. Tangle is the most emotionally complex "villain" in any arc — a creature that wanted to create beauty and accidentally caused a crisis.

**Key recurring elements:**  
- Chief Pip and Branchwick (Issues 1, 7 — bookends)
- Tangle (appears from Issue 1 edges; fully revealed in Issue 6; partner in Issue 7)
- The "fact family" tradition — every division problem produces its multiplication family (series habit)
- "Remainder culture" of Split Ridge (Issues 5, 6)
- The number 12 — Tangle's favourite number, woven through all its sculptures
