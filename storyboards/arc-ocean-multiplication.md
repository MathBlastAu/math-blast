## World Bible — The Luminous Deep

The Luminous Deep is an underwater world that exists far below the surface of a vast alien ocean. It is home to the **Coralfolk** — small, intelligent crustacean-like beings who build glowing cities from living coral and communicate through patterns of bioluminescent light. Their civilisation runs on a system called **the Lattice** — an ancient grid of glowing signal stones embedded across the ocean floor that transmits light-messages between cities.

The Lattice works on multiplication: each signal stone amplifies a message by sending it to a fixed number of neighbouring stones. A signal sent to 3 stones, each of which sends to 3 more, reaches 9 stones — then 27, then 81. The Coralfolk have used this pattern for generations to coordinate harvests, warn of danger, and share knowledge. They call it "the ripple."

But something has gone wrong. The signals are arriving wrong — some cities are getting too many messages, others none at all. And a deep, rhythmic hum has started echoing through the ocean floor — coming from somewhere below the Lattice itself.

**Visual palette:** Deep indigo and midnight blue water, vivid coral in orange/pink/yellow, bioluminescent greens and teals, golden signal stones, shafts of filtered light from above. Rich, symbol-free, stunning AI imagery.

### MARINA — Lead Character (Human)

**Age:** 10  
**Role:** Junior Ocean Researcher / Explorer  
**Physical description:**  
- Mixed-heritage girl, warm brown skin, dark brown eyes with a curious, sharp quality  
- Dark hair pulled back in a practical bun, with a few loose strands  
- Wears a slim-fit ocean research suit in deep teal, with white trim and a circular emblem (a wave pattern) on the chest  
- Helmet: a clear dome visor attached to a compact breather unit worn at the collar — sits back on her head when not in use  
- Carries a wrist-mounted datapad that glows soft amber  
- Build: lean and capable, a swimmer's build  

**Personality:** Methodical and curious — she maps things before she moves, counts before she guesses. Not timid, but she thinks first. Strong empathy; the Coralfolk trust her quickly because she listens before she talks.

### THE HUM — Arc Antagonist (revealed gradually)

**Nature:** Not a villain — a massive, ancient creature called a **Resonance Whale**. The largest living creature in the Luminous Deep, it has been dormant for centuries. It has woken up, and its natural bioelectric field is interfering with the Lattice's signal stones.  
**Physical description:**  
- Enormous — only partially visible in any single image; always implied rather than fully shown until Issue 4  
- Dark charcoal grey body, almost black  
- Covered in patches of bioluminescent blue-green spots arranged in grid-like rows — these are its natural pattern, but they happen to pulse in mathematical sequences  
- Eyes: large, gentle, deep violet  
- No mouth visible in normal shots; the "hum" comes from vibrations in its entire body  
- Presence: majestic and awe-inspiring, not threatening  

**Reveal arc:** Issue 1 = only the hum felt. Issue 2 = a shadow and a distant glow. Issue 3 = partial view (a fin, a patch of spots). Issue 4 = full reveal, awe moment.  
**Voice for prompting:** "Enormous ancient whale, dark charcoal body, bioluminescent blue-green spots in grid-like rows, large gentle violet eyes, partially visible through dark water, majestic and awe-inspiring"

## Issue-by-Issue Story Outline

---

### Issue 1 — "Into the Deep"
**Title:** The Lattice is Broken  
**Math:** Equal groups, "groups of", introduction to multiplication  
**Setting:** Entry point — the Research Sub, then first contact with the Coralfolk city of Luminos  

**Story:**  
Marina and Flick are on a routine research dive when a distress signal (a strange pattern of flashing lights) leads them to Luminos — the main Coralfolk city. Elder Luma explains: the Lattice should send each signal to 3 stones, each of which sends to 3 more — "groups of 3." But signals are arriving in wrong groups. Marina needs to understand how the Lattice works before she can help fix it.

**Math thread:** The Coralfolk demonstrate how "groups of" works — 3 fish pens with 4 fish in each. Marina works through problems with Flick.  
**Cliffhanger:** The hum begins — deep, rhythmic — and the Lattice flickers. Something big is down there.  
**Blast from the Past:** None (Issue 1).

---

### Issue 2 — "The Ripple Pattern"
**Title:** Skip the Count  
**Math:** Skip counting as multiplication (×2, ×5, ×10), connecting counting patterns to times tables  
**Setting:** The outer Lattice fields — vast grids of glowing signal stones across the ocean floor  

**Story:**  
Marina and Flick follow a Coralfolk Lattice-keeper named Wren (young, nervous, eager) out to the outer stones to trace the signal fault. The stones are arranged in rows — skip counting the glow patterns reveals where the disruption started. As they trace it, a massive shadow passes overhead.

**Math thread:** Wren teaches Marina how the Coralfolk count their stone grids by skipping — "count every second stone, then every fifth." Marina solves which section of the grid is misfiring.  
**Cliffhanger:** The shadow returns. Wren says no creature that size has been seen in living memory.  
**Blast from the Past:** "Groups of" from Issue 1 — how many stones send signals in each group?

---

### Issue 3 — "Rows and Columns"
**Title:** The Array  
**Math:** Arrays — rows × columns, commutativity  
**Setting:** The Archive — an ancient Coralfolk library built inside a massive shipwreck  

**Story:**  
Elder Luma leads Marina to the Archive to find records of the last time the hum was heard — centuries ago. The records are stored on stone tablets arranged in a massive grid. To find the right tablet fast, Marina has to navigate by rows and columns. She discovers that 3 rows of 4 tablets is the same number as 4 rows of 3 — the Coralfolk have known this for centuries and call it "the mirror truth." She finds the record — and reads about the Resonance Whale.

**Math thread:** Rows and columns of tablets as arrays. Marina realises that the Whale's bioluminescent spots are arranged in a grid too — and the grid is pulsing in multiplication patterns.  
**Cliffhanger:** A partial view — a vast fin, rows of glowing spots — passes the Archive window. It's close.  
**Blast from the Past:** Skip counting — how many tablets in 5 rows of 6?

---

### Issue 4 — "The Great Hum"
**Title:** Wake the Whale  
**Math:** Larger multiplication (×6, ×7, ×8), applying multiplication to solve a real problem  
**Setting:** The Deep Trench — below the Lattice, open water, the Whale's resting ground  

**Story:**  
Marina and Flick descend to meet the Resonance Whale. Full reveal — enormous, awe-inspiring, gentle. Its bioelectric pulse is disrupting the Lattice, but it's not intentional — the Whale is confused and distressed after centuries of sleep. Elder Luma explains the Lattice can be recalibrated if Marina can calculate the correct signal frequency — which requires working out the Whale's pulse pattern (multiples of 6, 7, and 8).

Marina solves the calculation, Flick carries the signal to the right stones, the Lattice reactivates — and the Whale, calmed by the harmonised signal, drifts peacefully into deeper water. The Coralfolk light up the city in celebration.

**Math thread:** Whale's pulse pattern = multiplication arrays of 6, 7, 8. Marina works through the calculations with the reader's help.  
**Cliffhanger:** None — satisfying resolution. Post-credit: Elder Luma hands Marina a signal stone as a gift. "When you need us, you know how to call."  
**Blast from the Past:** Arrays from Issue 3 — the Whale's spots as rows × columns.
