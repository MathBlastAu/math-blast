🌊 Chapter 1 — The Signal

Something in the Dark Was Calling for Help.

Press ▶ to hear the story
Marina pressed her nose against the cold porthole of the research sub. Outside, the ocean was endless midnight blue, so deep it seemed solid. Flick hovered beside her, antenna fins pointing straight up with curiosity. They had been diving for three hours when the signal found them.

It began as a faint pattern of flashing lights, pulsing through the dark water. Flick's wing-tips flared bright cyan. This was no ordinary light. Marina checked her amber datapad. The pattern was repeating, and it was mathematical. She set a new course, following the signal.

What appeared from the darkness took Marina's breath away. A city. Built entirely from living coral, glowing in shades of orange and pink and teal. Streets of glowing light, and creatures moving purposefully along them. The great Coralfolk city of Luminos. And it was sending a distress call.

🦀 Chapter 2 — Luminos

The Lattice Keeper Had Been Waiting.

Press ▶ to hear the story
A small creature approached the sub's airlock, moving with deliberate, measured steps. Its shell was deep burgundy-red, etched with geometric patterns. It carried a black coral staff topped with a glowing amber stone. This was Elder Luma, keeper of the Lattice.

"You came. The Lattice sensed you were different. A mathematical mind. We have been sending distress signals for three tide-cycles, hoping someone would understand the pattern. Something is wrong with the Lattice, and I fear we cannot fix it alone."

Elder Luma led Marina through the glowing streets of Luminos. The Coralfolk, small crab-like beings no taller than Marina's knee, stepped aside with respectful nods. Flick darted ahead, delighted by everything.

💡 Chapter 3 — The Ripple

The Lattice Runs on Groups of the Same Size.

Press ▶ to hear the story
Elder Luma sat beside the Lattice stones and explained how they worked. Each stone could send a signal to its neighbouring stones, and the number of neighbours it signalled was always the same. Equal groups, every time.

"We call it the Ripple," said Elder Luma, in a deep, considered voice. "One stone sends to three. Those three each send to three more. Always the same number. Always in equal groups. This is what makes the Lattice reliable. This is what has kept Luminos connected to all the other cities for a thousand tides."

Marina's eyes lit up. "So if every stone sends to the same number of other stones, the message spreads in a predictable pattern. Like multiplication. That's what you're using. Groups of the same size!"

📊 Chapter 4 — The Calculation

The More Marina Understood, the More Hope Elder Luma Had.

Press ▶ to hear the story
Marina pulled up her amber wrist datapad and began making notes. Flick hovered at her shoulder, wing-tips pulsing with steady cyan light. Each stone in the Lattice sent its signal to 4 neighbouring stones, without exception. Four groups of four. Elder Luma watched Marina work through the numbers, nodding slowly.

"You see it," said Elder Luma. "The Ripple is built entirely from equal groups. Every calculation in the Lattice is multiplication. If you understand groups of, you understand everything about how Luminos works. And perhaps, how it can be repaired."

Marina filled her datapad with calculations. Flick was nearly glowing with excitement, unable to stay still. The more problems Marina solved, the more Elder Luma's ancient amber eyes seemed to soften with hope.

⚡ Chapter 5 — The Hum Begins

Something Down There Was Waking Up.

Press ▶ to hear the story
Then something changed. A vibration moved through the ocean floor. Not a sound exactly, more of a feeling, a deep and rhythmic pulse that Marina felt in her chest before she heard it with her ears. The Lattice stones began to flicker. The Coralfolk around them froze, antenna fins pressed flat against their shells.

"The Hum," said Elder Luma, very quietly. "It has begun again. Something enormous is down there, in the deep water below the Lattice floor. We do not know what it is. We have not seen it. But we can feel it."

Marina stared down at the flickering stones. Her datapad was full of calculations. She understood groups of now. But whatever was making that hum, down in the darkness, she had a feeling the real work was just beginning.
