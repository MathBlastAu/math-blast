# Storyboard: Issue #1 — "Crash Landing on Planet Zog"

**Series:** Mission: Math Blast  
**Issue:** #1  
**Character:** Jake (Jake version; Blaze version not yet produced)  
**Math Topics:** Skip counting, times tables, fractions, telling the time, multiplication, division  
**ACARA Alignment:** Number & Measurement strands, Year 2–4  
**Format:** 5 chapters, 10 questions, interactive HTML

---

## Overall Story Premise

Jake, a trainee astronaut cadet, is cruising through the Zorblax Nebula when his rocket **crash-lands on Planet Zog**. To repair his rocket and escape before getting trapped in the Zog Gravitational Loop forever, Jake must collect fuel crystals, negotiate with friendly aliens, hit a precise launch window, navigate an asteroid belt, and finally execute a perfect re-entry sequence back to Earth. Math problems are his tools — without solving each one, he literally cannot proceed.

**Stakes:** If Jake misses the launch window, he's trapped on Zog for 47 years. If he can't navigate the asteroid belt, the rocket is destroyed. If he times re-entry wrong, the heat shield fails.

---

## Character Arc

Jake starts rattled — crashed and alone on an alien planet. Through each chapter he regains control: fixing the engine (Chapter 1), making alien friends (Chapter 2), mastering the clock (Chapter 3), surviving the asteroid belt (Chapter 4), and finally landing home triumphant (Chapter 5). He moves from reactive to confident.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Breakdown

### Chapter 1 — The Crashed Rocket ⚡
**Scene:** Jake's rocket smashes into Planet Zog. He stumbles out into alien terrain.  
**Story beat:** His training kicks in — run a pre-launch computer check before anything else.  
**Emotional tone:** Surprise + urgency. "How did this happen?! I need to fix it NOW."

| Q# | Label | Math Concept | ACARA | Difficulty | Story Integration |
|----|-------|--------------|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Q1 (Warm-Up) | Blast from the Past | Skip counting in 5s | Number, Y2 | 🔁 Retrieval | The mission computer runs a fuel reserve countdown: 5, 10, 15, ___, 25, ___ — Jake must complete it to pass the pre-launch check |
| Q2 | Times Tables | 6× multiplication (24 ÷ 6) | Number, Y3 | ⭐ Easy | Fuel crystals are scattered. Jake needs exactly 24 crystals in rows of 6 — how many rows to collect? |

**Narrative purpose of math:** The skip counting is a "computer boot sequence" — satisfying and low-stakes warm-up. The times tables question is the first real obstacle: Jake physically cannot launch without the right crystals.

---

### Chapter 2 — The Alien Feast 🍕
**Scene:** Three friendly Zog aliens — Bloop (tiny purple crown), Zibble, and Mork — intercept Jake. "You must share food before you leave. It is the Zog Law."  
**Story beat:** A glowing alien pizza appears. Equal shares are mandatory. Mork only eats half of his slice.  
**Emotional tone:** Comic relief. Aliens are ridiculous and loveable. Jake has to play along.

| Q# | Label | Math Concept | ACARA | Difficulty | Story Integration |
|----|-------|--------------|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Q3 | Fractions | Unit fractions — quarters | Number, Y2 | ⭐ Easy | 4 friends, 1 pizza, equal shares. What fraction each? |
| Q4 | Fractions | Fractions of fractions (½ of ¼) | Number, Y3–4 | ⭐⭐ Medium | Mork only eats half his quarter-slice. What fraction of the whole pizza did Mork eat? |

**Narrative purpose of math:** Fairness is the Zog Law — fractions are literally the cultural requirement here. Q4 is a stretch: the pizza model makes the abstract concrete (reader can visualise the slice being halved again).

---

### Chapter 3 — The Launch Window! 🕐
**Scene:** Mork grabs Jake's arm. "Leave too early or too late and you will be TRAPPED in the Zog Gravitational Loop for 47 years!"  
**Story beat:** Jake must read the cockpit clock exactly right, then calculate his departure deadline.  
**Emotional tone:** High stakes urgency. First introduction of the clock as a life-or-death instrument.

| Q# | Label | Math Concept | ACARA | Difficulty | Story Integration |
|----|-------|--------------|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Q5 | Telling the Time ✨ (New Concept) | Reading analogue clock to quarter-hour | Measurement, Y2 | ⭐⭐ Medium | Short hand past 3, long hand on 3 — what time does the clock show? Jake MUST read this right. |
| Q6 | Adding Time | Adding 30 minutes to a given time | Measurement, Y3 | ⭐⭐ Medium | It's 3:15. Window closes in 30 minutes. What time must Jake launch by? |

**Narrative purpose of math:** The clock is already in the cockpit. Reading it wrong = 47 years stuck. The stakes make Q5 feel essential, not academic. Q6 flows directly from the answer — readers carry 3:15 forward into the calculation.

---

### Chapter 4 — The Asteroid Belt 🌌
**Scene:** Jake blasts off just in time. Ahead: the Zorblax Asteroid Belt — spinning space rocks every pilot dreads.  
**Story beat:** Jake calculates his exact route through the belt, then does final accounts of his star-coin rewards.  
**Emotional tone:** Action and danger. "WHOOSH!" energy — the reader is in the cockpit.

| Q# | Label | Math Concept | ACARA | Difficulty | Story Integration |
|----|-------|--------------|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Q7 | Multiplication | 7× tables (6 × 7) | Number, Y3 | ⭐⭐ Medium | 6 asteroids, each 7 light-seconds wide — total distance Jake must cross? Navigation depends on the answer. |
| Q8 | Division | Equal sharing (36 ÷ 4) | Number, Y3 | ⭐⭐⭐ Hard | Jake has 36 star-coins for his 4 Zogling friends — how many coins each? (He calculates en route.) |

**Narrative purpose of math:** Q7 is navigation — wrong answer = wrong course. Q8 is a softer moment mid-danger: the fairness of sharing with friends, even while dodging asteroids. Nice tonal contrast.

---

### Chapter 5 — Home At Last! 🔥 (Boss Round)
**Scene:** Earth is visible through the cockpit window. Mission Control radios two final anomalies: solve them to clear the re-entry path.  
**Story beat:** Jake pulls out his notebook one last time. Two problems. Then home. Then dinner.  
**Emotional tone:** Climax of anticipation. Payoff is close — the reader feels the finish line.

| Q# | Label | Math Concept | ACARA | Difficulty | Story Integration |
|----|-------|--------------|-------|------------|-------------------|
| Q9 | Multi-Step | (4 × 7) + 3 | Number, Y3–4 | ⭐⭐⭐ Hard | Jake passes 4 space stations; 7 rockets join at each, plus 3 he started with. Total convoy size? Mission Control needs the number. |
| Q10 | BOSS: Subtracting Time 👑 | Reverse elapsed time | Measurement, Y3–4 | 👑 Boss | Re-entry window opens at 4:10. Heat shield warm-up takes 35 minutes. What time must Jake START? |

**Narrative purpose of math:** Q9 tests multi-step thinking — a real-world logistics problem (convoy size for safety). Q10 is the single most important reverse-engineering problem: work backwards from a deadline. Get it wrong, heat shield fails. The Boss framing makes it feel like a final game level.

---

## Resolution

Jake lands perfectly on Earth. The blue-green marble fills the cockpit window. Mission Control cheers. Jake scores his mission debrief. **The reader gets a "What You Mastered Today" summary** — a moment of genuine pride. No cliffhanger in Issue 1 (it's the first issue; needs a clean win to hook readers).

**Teaser at the end:** "Jake discovers a robot factory hidden in the rings of Saturn..."

## Pedagogical Notes

- **CPA approach:** Fractions shown via pizza model (pictorial). Time shown via clock face (pictorial → abstract).
- **Misconception targets:** Q4 addresses the "half of a quarter = a quarter" misconception. Q10 addresses "add time forward instead of subtract" tendency.
- **Difficulty ramp:** Q1 (Year 2) → Q10 (Year 4). Warm start, earn the Boss.
- **Spaced retrieval:** Q1 is "Blast from the Past" — a Year 2 skill — to prime the brain.

## What Works / Notes for Future Issues
