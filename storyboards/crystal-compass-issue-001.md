**MLC Value:** Integrity — things must be in their right place  
**Y2 Maths:** Place value — tens and ones (numbers to 100)

## Chapter Breakdown

---

### Chapter 1 — The Old Box

**Setting:** School library, after class. Warm golden light. Quiet.

**Story:**
Miss Zara sends the four girls to return some books after school.
The library is empty and cosy.
Priya spots something on a dusty shelf.
It's an old wooden box with a strange glow coming from inside.
Isla reaches for it straight away.
Zoe says, "Wait. We should open it carefully."
Inside is a beautiful compass. It glows with soft cyan light.
The needle spins and spins. It won't stop.

**Q1 — Warm Up (Place value intro):**
The compass face shows a number. It has 2 tens and 3 ones.
*What number is that?* **(23)**

*Why it fits:* The girls are meeting the compass for the first time. The first puzzle is gentle — just reading a number from tens and ones.

**Image scene:** Four girls huddled around an open wooden box on a library table. Soft cyan glow lighting their faces. Warm amber library behind them.

---

### Chapter 2 — Something Is Wrong

**Setting:** Library, same moment.

**Story:**
Amara picks up the compass. The glow flickers.
Numbers appear on the face — but they look wrong.
The digits are all mixed up. Out of order.
Zoe studies it closely. "I think I understand," she says.
"The numbers need to be in the right place."
"Tens go here. Ones go here."
"If they're in the wrong place — the whole number changes."

**Q2:**
The compass shows 4 tens and 7 ones.
*What number is that?* **(47)**

**Q3:**
Now it shows 7 tens and 4 ones.
*What number is that?* **(74)**
*Is 74 bigger or smaller than 47?* **(Bigger)**

*Why it fits:* Q2 and Q3 are the same digits — different places, different numbers. This is the core insight of place value, and it mirrors Zoe's "things must be in the right place" moment.

**Image scene:** Zoe holding the compass up, studying it with a focused expression. The compass face glows with numbers. Other girls watching.

---

### Chapter 3 — Learning the Language

**Setting:** Library. The girls sit at a table together.

**Story:**
Priya pulls out her notebook. She loves patterns.
"Let's figure out the rules," she says.
They test numbers one by one.
Every time the tens and ones are right — the compass glows brighter.
Every time they mix them up — the light fades.
"It's like a language," says Priya. "Numbers have an order."
Amara grins. "Then we need to learn to speak it."

**Q4:**
The compass shows 5 tens and 0 ones.
*What number is that?* **(50)**

**Q5:**
It shows 0 tens and 8 ones.
*What number is that?* **(8)**

**Q6:**
Now it shows 6 tens and 3 ones.
*What number is that?* **(63)**

*Why it fits:* Three quick questions in a row, each slightly different — including zero in the tens place and zero in the ones place, which trips kids up.

**Image scene:** Priya writing in her notebook at the library table. Compass glowing in the centre. All four girls looking engaged and curious.

---

### Chapter 4 — The Test (⚡ SPEED ROUND)

**Setting:** Library. The compass is glowing stronger now — then it starts to flicker.

**Story:**
Suddenly the compass light begins to fade.
The glow pulses — weaker and weaker.
"It's going out!" cries Amara.
Numbers flash on the face — fast, urgent.
"It needs answers," says Zoe. "Right now. Quickly!"
The compass is fading. There's no time to think too long.

**⚡ SPEED ROUND — 5 questions, 8 seconds each**
*UI: urgent ticking sound, timer bar drains on screen, questions appear one by one*

**Q7:** Which is bigger — 38 or 83? **(83)**
**Q8:** 2 tens and 9 ones = ? **(29)**
**Q9:** Which is smaller — 61 or 16? **(16)**
**Q10:** 5 tens and 5 ones = ? **(55)**
**Q11 (bonus):** Put in order: 42, 24, 44. **(24, 42, 44)**

*Score shown after: "You answered X out of 5 before the light faded!"*
*Story continues regardless of score.*

The compass blazes gold. The needle locks in place.
It points straight at the far wall — at a door they never noticed before.

*Why it fits:* The fading compass creates genuine urgency. Questions are place value recall — same concept, faster pace. 8 seconds is tight but fair for Y2.

**Image scene:** The compass flickering, girls looking panicked. Numbers flashing rapidly on the compass face.

---

### Chapter 5 — The Door Opens

**Setting:** Library wall. A door that wasn't there before.

**Story:**
The girls walk slowly toward the door.
It's wooden, old, and covered in carved patterns.
The compass pulls toward it like a magnet.
Zoe reaches out and touches the handle.
It's warm.
She looks at the others. They nod.
She turns the handle. The door swings open.
Beyond it is a forest. But not a normal forest.
The trees glow. The leaves shimmer cyan and purple.
And the lights — they pulse. In a pattern.

**Q10 — Final challenge:**
Before they step through, the compass asks one last question.
What number is made of 10 tens? **(100)**

*Why it fits:* 100 = 10 tens is the capstone of place value at Y2 level. It's also a satisfying "big number" reveal before the adventure continues.

**Image scene (cliffhanger):** The library door open, four silhouettes of the girls standing at the threshold. Beyond the door — the glowing forest, cyan and purple light, pulsing in patterns. Wonder and excitement on their faces.

## Parent Guide (end of issue)

**What your child learned:**
Place value — the idea that where a digit sits changes its value completely. The 4 in 47 means 4 tens (forty). The 4 in 74 means 4 ones. This is one of the most important ideas in Year 2 maths.

**Try this at home:**
Write the number 36 on a piece of paper. Ask: "How many tens? How many ones?" Then swap the digits to make 63. Ask: "Is this bigger or smaller? Why?"
