## Characters

### Jake
10-year-old boy. Short messy brown hair, bright green eyes, freckles. Orange space suit with silver trim, blue visor helmet, red rocket patch. **Methodical, careful, notebook-checker.** Tends to draw diagrams and verify his working.

### Blaze
10-year-old girl. Short dark practical hair, warm olive/tan skin, ambiguous ethnicity. Teal/cyan jumpsuit with purple accents, lightning bolt badge. **Bold, instinctive, fearless.** Estimates first and refines. Moves fast and corrects on the fly.

Every issue offers the choice of Jake or Blaze — same plot, different character voice.

## Issue Structure (every issue)

Every issue follows the same format:
- **5 chapters**
- **10 questions** (Q1 = warm-up retrieval, Q10 = Boss Challenge)
- **ACARA-aligned** difficulty, ramping from ~Year 3 to ~Year 4
- **Cliffhanger** (Issues 1–6) or **Resolution** (Issue 7)
- **"Blast from the Past"** warm-up Q1 (spaced retrieval from prior issues)
- **Parent Guide** with curriculum mapping

## Arc Summaries

### 🚀 Space — Fractions ("The Galactic Fuel Crisis")
The Interstellar Fuel Network is failing — fractions of fuel are being stolen from every depot. Jake/Blaze is a Junior Fuel Inspector. The culprit: the **Fraction Phantom**, a rogue AI that genuinely believes it's being fair — it just doesn't understand that equal fractions don't mean equal real amounts.

**Math progression:** unit fractions → fractions of collections → equivalent fractions → number line → comparing fractions → mixed numbers/improper fractions → fractions as division

**Tone:** Wonder, problem-solving, moral complexity (the villain is sympathetic). The galaxy is spectacular.

---

### 🌿 Jungle — Division ("The Vanishing Village")
The Sprocket tribe — whose entire civilisation runs on equal sharing — is losing members. Their vine highways are being cut. The culprit: **Tangle**, a gentle vine-creature who collects beautiful patterns without realising it's isolating the villages.

**Math progression:** partitive division → quotitive division → fact families → remainders → remainder meaning in context → short division → division connecting to fractions

**Tone:** Warm, funny, morally rich. Division is the basis of the Sprockets' entire civilisation. Tangle is the most emotionally resonant "villain" in the series.

---

### 💎 Treasure Hunting — Equations ("The Scales of Fortune")
A 7-island treasure hunt through the ruins of the Scalan civilisation, whose locks require balanced equations. Rival: **Captain Crooke**, who thinks math is just "calculating stuff" and never understands the equals sign until the very end.

**Math progression:** equals sign as balance → true/false sentences → one-step equations → inverse operations → function machines → expressions on both sides → two-variable systems

**Tone:** Adventure/puzzle energy. Classic treasure hunt with intellectual locks. Crooke grows from antagonist to reluctant partner.

---

### 🏺 Pyramids — Time ("The Chronos Tombs")
Seven pyramids built by the Vel'Nara — an ancient time-obsessed civilisation. Every lock is a time problem. Rival: **Professor Tempo**, a bumbling archaeologist who can't tell the time properly (but gradually improves). The final issue involves resetting the civilisation's Master Clock — broken for 3,200 days.

**Math progression:** clock reading (to 5 min) → adding time intervals → elapsed time (open number lines) → timetables → 24-hour time → calendar calculations → multi-step time problems

**Tone:** Ancient, reverent, urgent. Time is the civilisation's religion. The bittersweet ending (missed the Vel'Nara descendants by 39 minutes) makes time feel genuinely consequential.

## Pedagogy Notes

### Core Principles Applied
- **CPA Approach** (Concrete → Pictorial → Abstract): every arc starts with concrete story contexts before moving to abstract notation
- **Spaced Retrieval**: every Q1 is a "Blast from the Past" reviewing prior content
- **Both Meanings of Division**: Jungle arc explicitly introduces both partitive and quotitive division
- **Relational Equals Sign**: Equations arc starts with true/false sentences to build correct understanding
- **Open Number Lines**: Pyramids arc uses open number lines for all elapsed time problems

### Key Misconceptions Targeted
| Misconception | Where Addressed |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Larger denominator = larger fraction | Space arc, Issue 1 Q7 |
| ½ of a quarter = a quarter (not 1/8) | Existing Issue #1, Q4 |
| Equals sign means "the answer comes next" | Equations arc, Issue 1 Q1–Q3 |
| Division always makes things smaller | Jungle arc, Issue 5–6 |
| Subtracting time = subtracting numbers | Pyramids arc throughout |
| Minutes hand showing 3 = 3 minutes | Pyramids arc Issue 1 |

## Production Notes

### Issue Numbering
Issues #1 and #2 exist (documented in `existing-issue-001.md` and `existing-issue-002.md`). The 4 arcs begin with Issue #3 and run through Issue #30. Issues can be released in parallel across arcs (e.g., Space #1, Jungle #1, Pyramid #1, Equations #1 in the same month) or sequentially.

### Recommended Release Strategy
Option A — **One arc at a time**: Run all 7 Space issues, then all 7 Jungle issues, etc. Readers master one topic deeply.

Option B — **Interleaved**: Release one issue from each arc per month. Readers experience variety and their subscription covers all four worlds.

### Character Choice Implementation
Each issue should open with a character selection screen (Jake or Blaze). The character name, pronoun, and dialogue style changes, but all math content is identical. Blaze version should subtly model estimation-then-refinement while Jake models diagram-first approach.
