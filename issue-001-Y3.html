The Interstellar Fuel Network kept every ship in the galaxy moving. Hundreds of stations, thousands of tanks — all tracked, all measured. Until now.

Jake Nova, Junior Fuel Inspector, docked at Kepler Station — a busy depot orbiting a red dwarf star at the edge of the sector. "Alright, alright — I've got this," he told himself. "First solo assignment. Let's see what we're dealing with."

Fuel Officer Priya met him at the airlock, arms crossed. "Inspector Nova. Our tanks are showing fraction readings and half my crew doesn't know what they mean. Three cargo ships are waiting to refuel. If we can't figure this out, their crews will be stranded in deep space."

Jake pulled out his notebook. "Fractions I can do. Let me start with my own ship's gauge."
