⚡ Chapter 1 — The Crashed Rocket

Jake's Fuel Crystals Are Scattered!

Jake the astronaut cadet was hurtling through the Zorblax Nebula — munching a space sandwich and humming to himself — when BANG! CRASH! CLONK!

His rocket had smashed straight into Planet Zog! Through the smoke and sparks, Jake stumbled out and inspected the damage. But first — his training kicked in. Before any mission, his computer always ran a Pre-Launch Check. Even crashed on an alien planet, the rules were the rules.

🍕 Chapter 2 — The Alien Feast

The Zoglings Want to Share!

Before Jake could fire up the engines, three small green aliens waddled over. The tallest one — wearing a tiny purple crown — bowed deeply.

"GREETINGS, SPACE HUMAN! I AM BLOOP. THESE ARE ZIBBLE AND MORK. WE MUST SHARE FOOD BEFORE YOU LEAVE. IT IS THE ZOG LAW."

A giant glowing pizza appeared from nowhere. There were 4 of them altogether, and Bloop insisted everyone got an equal share.

What fraction does each person get?

"MORK NOT HUNGRY. MORK EAT ONLY HALF OF MORK'S SLICE."

Jake wondered: if Mork's slice was one quarter of the whole pizza, and Mork only ate half of that — what fraction of the whole pizza did Mork eat?

✨ New This Issue

Telling the Time — reading an analog clock to the quarter hour, and adding/subtracting 30-minute intervals. This is a Year 2–3 ACARA Measurement skill.

🔁 Spaced Review

Q1 warmed up skip counting in 5s — a Year 2 Number skill. Future issues will revisit topics from previous weeks to keep them fresh in memory.

💬 Conversation Starters

  • After Q4 (tricky fractions): "If you cut a quarter of a cake in half, how many pieces would you need to cut the whole cake into to make them all equal?"
  • After Q5–6 (time): "If dinner is at 6:00, and it takes 20 minutes to cook, what time should we start?" — use a real clock if you can!
  • After Q10 (boss): "How did you work backwards from 4:10? Can you show me on paper?"
  • Any time: "Which question was hardest? Can you explain why?"

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Watch For

  • Fractions of fractions (Q4): Kids often think "half of a quarter = a quarter". Use physical objects — fold a piece of paper into quarters, then fold one quarter in half.
  • Clock reading (Q5): Confusing the short and long hands is common. Try: "Short = hours (because it doesn't travel far), Long = minutes (because it has a long way to go)."
  • Subtracting time (Q10): Many kids add instead of subtract when working with time. Ask: "Do we need to go forward or backward on the clock?"
  • Multi-step problems (Q9): Kids often forget the first step. Encourage them to underline each piece of information and number the steps before calculating.
