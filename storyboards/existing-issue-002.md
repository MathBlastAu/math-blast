# Storyboard: Issue #2 — "The Robot Factory"

**Series:** Mission: Math Blast  
**Issue:** #2  
**Character:** Jake (with robot sidekick Bolt)  
**Math Topics:** Fractions review, multiplication arrays, word problems, multi-digit multiplication, area  
**ACARA Alignment:** Number & Measurement strands, Year 3–4  
**Format:** 5 chapters, 10 questions, interactive HTML

---

## Overall Story Premise

Jake returns to space with his new robot co-pilot **Bolt** — a round, silver robot with glowing blue eyes that spin when he's excited. Bolt's sensors detect an alien structure hidden in Saturn's rings: a dormant robot factory, silent for centuries. Jake and Bolt dock and explore, and Jake must solve multiplication problems to count components, power the factory back up, manage its logistics, and ultimately unlock a special upgrade for Bolt. Math isn't decoration — every question is part of the activation sequence.

**Stakes:** Without the right numbers, the factory controller won't accept the input codes. Every wrong count could shut the whole factory down permanently. And Bolt's upgrade — the emotional heart of the issue — depends on solving the final Boss challenge.

---

## New Character: Bolt 🤖

- Round, silver body, glowing blue eyes that spin when excited

- Enthusiastic, loyal, slightly dramatic

**Emotional tone:** Overwhelming wonder. Hundreds of robots! Jake must stay calm and count accurately.

### Chapter 5 — Factory Online! 🔥 (Boss Round)
**Scene:** The factory roars to life! Every floor humming, every arm swinging, robots marching with purpose. Jake and Bolt reach the control room at the top.  
**Story beat:** Bolt's chest panel begins to glow — there's one more upgrade sequence, just for him. Two final challenges to unlock it.  
**Emotional tone:** Triumphant climax with warmth. The factory win is exciting; Bolt's upgrade is emotionally satisfying.

| Q# | Label | Math Concept | ACARA | Difficulty | Story Integration |
|----|-------|--------------|-------|------------|-------------------|

## Pedagogical Notes

- **Array progression:** Arrays start at 3×4 (familiar territory) and grow — 5×6, 4×7, then 12×3 and 24×2 (multi-digit). Excellent CPA progression.
- **Area connection:** Q9 explicitly introduces area as multiplication — a key Year 4 bridge.
- **Spaced retrieval:** Q1 reviews Issue #1 fractions — the "Blast from the Past" system working perfectly.
- **Word problem diversity:** Q5–Q6 are non-array word problems, testing whether students can construct the multiplication without a visual grid.
- **Boss challenge design:** (3×5) + (4×4) requires holding two separate calculations and combining — genuine multi-step thinking.
