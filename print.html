MATH BLAST

An adventure story where YOU solve the puzzles!

How to use this booklet:
Read the story together, then answer the maths puzzle at the end of each chapter. The correct answer is shown at the bottom of each quiz — try to solve it first before peeking! ⭐
⚡ Chapter 1 — The Fuel Crystals

Jake's Fuel Crystals Are Scattered!

Jake the astronaut cadet was flying his rocket through the Zorblax Nebula when — CRASH! — he made an emergency landing on Planet Zog!

He clambered out, dusted himself off, and checked the engine room. Oh no. The fuel crystals had scattered everywhere! He needed exactly 24 crystals to launch — and they came in rows of 6.

"How many rows do I need to collect?" Jake muttered, pulling out his maths notebook...
💎
Times Tables Challenge
Jake needs 24 fuel crystals. They come in rows of 6.
How many rows does he need?
3 rows
✅ 4 rows
5 rows
6 rows
✅ Answer: 4 rows Because 6 × 4 = 24! Jake scooped up all four rows of crystals and loaded them into the engine. The rocket hummed back to life!
🍕 Chapter 2 — The Alien Feast

The Zoglings Want to Share Their Pizza!

Jake had just finished loading the crystals when three friendly green aliens waddled over. Their names were Bloop, Zibble, and Mork.

"WELCOME TO ZOG! WE SHARE FOOD NOW!" announced Bloop, plonking a giant pizza on the ground.

There were 4 of them altogether — Jake, Bloop, Zibble, and Mork. They wanted to split the pizza into equal pieces so everyone got a fair share.

"If the whole pizza is 1," Jake said, "what fraction does each of us get?"
🍕
Fractions Challenge
4 friends share 1 pizza equally.
What fraction does each person get?
1/2
1/3
✅ 1/4
1/6
✅ Answer: 1/4 (one quarter) 1 pizza ÷ 4 friends = ¼ each. Everyone got a quarter of the pizza. Bloop did a little happy dance. 💃
🕐 Chapter 3 — The Launch Window!

The Rocket Must Launch at Exactly the Right Time!

Jake raced back to his rocket, tummy full of alien pizza, crystals loaded.

"WAIT!" called Mork. "You must leave during the Launch Window or you'll get stuck in the Zog Gravitational Loop FOREVER!"

Jake looked at the cockpit clock. The short hand was pointing just past the 3, and the long hand was pointing at the 3.

"Okay," Jake said. "I know exactly what time it is. Time to go home!"
🕐
Telling the Time Challenge
The short hand is just past the 3.
The long hand is pointing at the 3.

What time is it?
3:00
✅ 3:15
3:30
3:45
✅ Answer: 3:15 (quarter past three) When the long hand points to the 3, it means 15 minutes past the hour — quarter past! Jake hit the launch button and blasted off into space! 🚀
