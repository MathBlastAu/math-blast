# The Crystal Compass — Issue 4: "The Village Needs Help"

**MLC Value:** Humanity — caring for others, sharing fairly  
**Y2 Maths:** Measurement — length, mass, capacity  
**Chapters:** 5  
**Quiz questions:** 10  
**Audience:** Year 2 girls, ~7 years old  
**Writing rules:** Max 10–12 words per sentence. Simple words only.

---

## Characters This Issue
- **Amara** — leads this issue (Humanity is her value)
- **Isla, Priya, Zoe** — present throughout
- **Village characters** — kind but confused villagers needing help

---

## Chapter Breakdown

---

### Chapter 1 — Into the Village

**Setting:** The magical village. Cobblestone streets. Glowing lanterns. But something is off.

**Story:**
The girls climb down from the mountain.
The village is warm and beautiful.
But something is clearly wrong.
A bridge is too short to cross the stream.
A bucket overflows — it's too big for the shelf.
A package won't fit through the door.
A villager rushes over. "Oh thank goodness! Can you help us?"
Amara steps forward. "Of course. Tell us everything."

**Q1 — Warm Up:**
The bridge is 12 steps long. The stream is 15 steps wide.
*Is the bridge long enough?* **(No)**
*How many steps short is it?* **(3)**

*Why it fits:* Introduces measurement as a practical problem. Simple subtraction applied to length — eases into the topic.

**Image scene:** The four girls arriving in the magical village. A villager gestures to a too-short bridge. Wonky objects everywhere. Warm glowing lanterns above.

---

### Chapter 2 — The Bridge Problem

**Setting:** The village stream. A wooden bridge sits next to it — clearly too short.

**Story:**
The villagers need to cross the stream every day.
But the bridge was built without measuring.
Amara has an idea.
"We measure first. Then we build the right length."
She pulls a measuring tape from the compass bag.
"Measuring isn't hard," she says.
"You just have to care enough to do it right."

**Q2:**
The stream is 20 cm wide on the map. The bridge is 14 cm.
*How much longer does the bridge need to be?* **(6 cm)**

**Q3:**
A plank is 35 cm long. Another is 27 cm.
*How long are they together?* **(62 cm)**

**Q4:**
They need a bridge 60 cm long. They have 38 cm of wood.
*How much more wood do they need?* **(22 cm)**

*Why it fits:* Length measurement with addition and subtraction in context. The numbers are manageable but require thought.

**Image scene:** Amara measuring the stream with a tape measure, focused expression. Priya writing down numbers. Villagers watching hopefully.

---

### Chapter 3 — The Market Stall

**Setting:** Village market. Stalls with fruit, buckets, and bags — all the wrong sizes.

**Story:**
Next problem — the market is a mess.
Bags are too heavy to carry. Buckets are the wrong size.
Nobody knows what holds what.
Zoe takes charge. "We need to compare everything."
"Heavier. Lighter. More. Less."
"Once we know — we can sort it all out."
The compass hums with approval.

**Q5:**
A bag of apples weighs 4 kg. A bag of oranges weighs 7 kg.
*Which is heavier?* **(Oranges)**
*How much heavier?* **(3 kg)**

**Q6:**
A bucket holds 10 cups of water. It has 6 cups in it.
*How many more cups fit?* **(4)**

**Q7:**
Order these from lightest to heaviest: 5 kg, 2 kg, 8 kg.
**(2 kg, 5 kg, 8 kg)**

*Why it fits:* Moves from length to mass and capacity. Q7 asks for ordering — applying comparison, not just arithmetic.

**Image scene:** Zoe at a market stall comparing bags and buckets. Scales and measuring cups on the table. Villagers watching.

---

### Chapter 4 — Everyone Helps (⚡ SPEED ROUND)

**Setting:** All around the village — and a flood warning bell starts ringing.

**Story:**
There is so much to fix. They split up.
Then — a loud bell rings across the village.
A villager runs past. "The river is rising! We have one hour!"
"We have to fix everything NOW," says Amara.
No more careful checking. Just measure, solve, go.
The girls race across the village, tools in hand.

**⚡ SPEED ROUND — 5 questions, 8 seconds each**
*UI: bell ringing, water level gauge rising on side of screen, timer counts down*

**Q8:** A doorway is 90 cm. A package is 74 cm. How much space to spare? **(16 cm)**
**Q9:** A shelf holds 15 kg. It has 9 kg on it. How much more fits? **(6 kg)**
**Q10:** A plank is 45 cm. They need 60 cm. How much more? **(15 cm)**
**Q11:** A bucket holds 12 cups. It has 5. How many more fit? **(7)**
**Q12:** Two planks: 18 cm and 22 cm. How long together? **(40 cm)**

*Score shown after: "You answered X out of 5 before the flood arrived!"*
*Story continues regardless of score.*

The last fix is made just as the water laps at the village edge.
The new bridge holds. The water flows safely underneath.
The village is safe.

*Why it fits:* Flood urgency on the final issue feels earned — the biggest stakes of the arc. Measurement questions in rapid-fire context reinforces the whole issue's theme.

**Image scene:** The girls racing through the village, each fixing something fast. A water gauge on the village wall rising in the background. Urgency and determination on their faces.

---

### Chapter 5 — The Compass Glows White

**Setting:** Village square. All the villagers gathered. Late afternoon golden light.

**Story:**
All the problems are solved.
The village looks beautiful. Everything fits.
The villagers gather in the square.
They clap and cheer for the four girls.
Amara's cheeks go warm. She didn't do it for the clapping.
She did it because it was the right thing to do.
The compass glows — not gold this time. Bright white.
Brighter than it has ever glowed before.

**Q10 — Final challenge:**
The villagers want to share 100 apples equally between 4 families.
*How many apples does each family get?* **(25)**

*Why it fits:* Introduces very simple division as sharing equally — a natural extension of Humanity/fairness. Also previews the next maths concept for Y3 without overwhelming Y2 kids.

**Image scene:** All four girls in the village square, compass blazing brilliant white. Villagers cheering around them. Golden afternoon light. Warm, joyful ending.

---

**Final moment — the compass points home:**
The compass spins one last time. It points back toward the glowing forest — back the way they came.
The girls look at each other.
"Same time next week?" says Isla.
They all laugh.

## Parent Guide

**What your child learned:**
Measurement — comparing and calculating length, mass, and capacity. The key idea: measurement lets us solve real problems. You measure first, then you act.

**Try this at home:**
Find three objects around the house. Guess which is heaviest, then check with kitchen scales. Were you right? Try the same with length using a ruler.
