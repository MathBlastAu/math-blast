🔁 Chapter 1 — Approaching Saturn

Jake and Bolt Head for the Rings!

✨ New This Issue

Area as Multiplication — understanding that the area of a rectangle = length × width. A Year 4 Measurement skill. Use floor tiles, graph paper, or a room at home to make it concrete!

🔁 Spaced Review

Q1 revisited fractions from Issue #1. This spaced repetition approach helps lock in learning. Ask: "Do you remember how fractions worked from last week?"

💬 Conversation Starters

  • After Q2–Q4 (arrays): "Can you draw a 4×6 array? What about 6×4 — is it the same?" (Commutative property)
  • After Q7–Q8 (multi-digit): "How did you work out 12×3? Did you use a trick?" (Breaking into 10×3 + 2×3 = 36)
  • After Q9 (area): "What's the area of this table in hand-spans?" — actually measure it!
  • After Q10 (boss): "How many steps did this question have? Write them out — can you do it without a calculator?"

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Watch For

  • Arrays (Q2–Q4): Kids sometimes add instead of multiply rows × columns. Reinforce: "rows times columns = total"
  • Multi-digit × 1-digit (Q7–Q8): Carrying errors are common. Encourage working it out step by step on paper.
  • Area (Q9): Confusing area with perimeter (adding sides vs multiplying). Tip: "Area fills the inside — we multiply."
  • Multi-step (Q10): Not writing down sub-answers. Encourage writing: "Floor 1 = ___. Floor 2 = ___. Total = ___."
