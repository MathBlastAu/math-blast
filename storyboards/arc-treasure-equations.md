# Arc Storyboard: Treasure Hunting — Balancing Equations
## "The Scales of Fortune"

**World:** The Lost Archipelago — a chain of mysterious islands scattered across a shimmering sea, each holding part of a legendary treasure  
**Math Topic:** Balancing Equations (relational equals sign, open sentences, inverse operations, two-step equations)  
**ACARA Content:** AC9M3A01, AC9M3A02 → AC9M4A01, AC9M5A01 (Year 3 → Year 4/5)  
**Issues:** 7  
**Character Choice:** Jake (systematic, code-cracking approach) or Blaze (intuitive, "I feel the balance" approach)

---

## Arc Overview

### The Big Story
An ancient civilisation — the **Scalans** — once ruled the Lost Archipelago. They were master traders and builders, but their entire culture was built around one sacred principle: **perfect balance**. Every trade, every building, every door lock used the balance equation as its foundation. When they disappeared 2,000 years ago, they left behind an enormous treasure — but protected it with equation locks on every door, every chest, every pathway. The treasure can only be opened by someone who truly understands that the equals sign means *balance*, not *answer*.

Jake/Blaze discovers the first island by accident and is immediately drawn into a seven-issue treasure hunt. Each island presents a more complex set of equation locks. The treasure isn't gold — it's the Scalans' entire library of knowledge: the most advanced mathematics ever discovered. Worth more than any gold.

### Villain/Antagonist  
**Captain Crooke** — a treasure hunter with a crew of 4 (comical, bungling). Crooke thinks math is just "adding stuff up until you get the right answer" — she never understands that the equations need to BALANCE. She's always just ahead of Jake/Blaze or just behind, stealing map pieces and accidentally solving some locks (because she accidentally balances things) while breaking others. Not truly dangerous — more like a competitor who creates chaos.

By Issue 7, Crooke has the final map piece but can't open the last vault because she can't balance a two-variable equation. She needs Jake/Blaze's help. They solve it together — and Crooke learns what the equals sign really means.

### How Balancing Equations Threads Through the Arc
- **Issues 1–2:** Equals sign as balance — true/false sentences, open sentences with one unknown
- **Issues 3–4:** Unknown in any position, inverse operations, bar models
- **Issues 5–6:** Both sides have operations, two-step equations, equations with expressions on both sides
- **Issue 7:** Two unknowns, systems of equations, "solve for both to open the vault"

### Jake vs Blaze Personality Differences
| Aspect | Jake | Blaze |
|--------|------|-------|
| Approach | "Let me write out both sides and check the balance" | "If I take this away from both sides, they should even out" |
| Relationship with Captain Crooke | Wary, strategic — keeps important clues secret | Openly competitive — won't let Crooke see she's ahead |
| Equation style | Draws a balance scale diagram | Uses the bar model — "this side, that side" |
| When stuck | Checks his workings systematically | Tries a number and adjusts ("too big, try smaller") |

## Issue 1 of 7 — "The Island of Balanced Scales"

**Title:** The First Lock  
**Setting:** Isla Balancia — the first island, an obvious first clue to the archipelago's secrets  
**Difficulty:** Year 3 entry — equals sign as balance, true/false sentences, one unknown

### Story Premise
Jake/Blaze washes up on Isla Balancia (or arrives by boat) and discovers a stone archway covered in ancient symbols. Between the archway and the treasure cave beyond it stands an enormous golden balance scale — the Scalan Lock. The scale has pans on either side. Numbers and boxes appear on the pans. The scale only unlocks when both sides are equal.

**Stakes:** Captain Crooke arrives on the island at the same time. She's trying to force the lock — it's not working. Whoever solves it first gets the first map piece.

### Chapter Breakdown

**Chapter 1 — The Balance Arch**
*Setup:* Jake/Blaze finds the archway. Carved above it: "BALANCE IS TRUTH." The golden scale sits in the path.

*Q1 (Warm-Up):* The first scale shows: **4 + 2 on the left pan. 6 on the right pan. Is this balanced? True or false?** (True — 4 + 2 = 6). Understanding the equals sign as balance — not "the answer comes next."

**Chapter 2 — The Scalan Code**
*Rising action:* The scale presents three more sentences. Jake/Blaze must judge each one.

*Q2:* **5 + 3 = 9. True or false?** (False — 5 + 3 = 8, not 9). Discuss: "What would make it true?"

*Q3:* **The scale shows: 7 = 3 + □. What goes in the box to balance it?** (4). Crucial — the unknown is on the right, and the equals sign is "backwards" (right-to-left). This addresses the "answer comes after =" misconception.

**Chapter 3 — The Missing Weight**
*Midpoint:* The scale has harder weights now. Captain Crooke is watching — she writes down "7 = 3 + 4... so the box is 4... wait, that doesn't make sense, the answer should be on the right!" She's confused.

*Q4:* **□ + 6 = 13. What is □?** (7). Unknown on the left.

*Q5:* **9 = □ + 4. What is □?** (5). Unknown anywhere — cementing that balance works both ways.

**Chapter 4 — Both Sides Active**
*Climax:* The hardest lock. Both sides of the scale have operations.

*Q6:* **3 + 4 = 5 + □. What is □?** (2). Both sides must equal 7.

*Q7:* **□ + 6 = 4 + 7. What is □?** (5). Both sides must equal 11.

*Q8:* **2 + □ = 3 + 5. What is □?** (6). Build confidence with three of these in a row.

**Chapter 5 — The First Map Piece**
*Resolution:* The scale unlocks! A stone compartment opens. Inside: a rolled map fragment and a small golden weight engraved "PART 1 OF 7."

*Q9:* The map fragment shows a code: **4 × □ = 20. What is □?** (5 — extends balance to multiplication)

*Q10 (Boss):* **3 × □ = □ + 10. Both sides must be equal. What is □?** (5 — because 3 × 5 = 15 and 5 + 10 = 15)

**Cliffhanger:** Crooke grabs the map piece and runs. But Jake/Blaze noticed her write something down wrong — she copied the code incorrectly. She thinks the next island is to the north. Jake/Blaze knows it's to the east.

## Issue 2 of 7 — "The Trade Island"

**Title:** Equations at the Market  
**Setting:** Isla Mercado — an island where ancient Scalan trade records are carved everywhere; a crumbling market full of equation-based price locks  
**Difficulty:** Year 3 — one-step equations, inverse operations introduced, bar models

### Story Premise
Isla Mercado's market stalls are locked with equation prices. To "buy" each item (actually unlock each chest containing map pieces and clues), Jake/Blaze must balance the equation. But someone — Crooke's crew — has been scratching out some of the numbers, leaving only boxes. Jake/Blaze has to solve equations with missing information.

**Stakes:** The complete map to Island 3 is spread across 4 locked chests. Each chest has a different equation lock.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Market stall 1. Q1 (warm-up): **n + 8 = 15. Find n.** (7 — first use of a letter for the unknown). Jake/Blaze notes: "n is just like a box — it's a mystery number we're finding."

**Ch2:** Chests 2 and 3. Q2: **18 − n = 11. Find n.** (7 — subtraction equation). Q3: **n − 5 = 9. Find n.** (14 — unknown is the starting number, inverse reasoning required)

**Ch3:** Crooke's crew has made a mess of stall 4. Q4: Equation reads: **n × 4 = 28. Find n.** (7 — first multiplication equation; inverse = division). Q5: **3 × n = 27. Find n.** (9 — same structure, builds fluency)

**Ch4:** The hardest market lock. Q6: **n × 6 = 54. Find n.** (9). Q7: Bar model problem — the bar shows a total of 30 split into 5 equal sections, one section is labelled n. **Find n.** (6 — bar model as visual equation). Q8: **48 ÷ n = 6. Find n.** (8 — division equation with unknown divisor)

**Ch5:** All four chests opened. Q9: The map fragment has one more lock: **4 × n + 2 = 22. Find n.** (5 — two-step equation, first introduction). Q10 (Boss): **Two equations — same n: n + 7 = 15 and n × 2 = 16. Does the same n satisfy both? Find n.** (n = 8 from first; n = 8 from second — yes! N = 8 satisfies both. This plants the seed of systems.)

**Cliffhanger:** Crooke arrives with a partial map, realises Jake/Blaze has the other pieces. She holds up her fragment: "I propose a trade — half my map for half yours." It's a classic equation: half + half = whole. Except Crooke's "half" might not actually be half...

## Issue 3 of 7 — "The Function Machine Island"

**Title:** The Machine That Changes Numbers  
**Setting:** Isla Machina — an island with ancient Scalan mechanical contraptions; devices that take input numbers and transform them according to hidden rules  
**Difficulty:** Year 3–4 — function machines, working backwards, two-step equations

### Story Premise
The Scalans' greatest invention was the Function Machine — a device that takes a number, does something to it, and produces an output. The input-output pairs are carved on the side. Jake/Blaze must deduce the rule to open each machine's compartment. Crooke is operating one machine randomly, inputting numbers and hoping for the right output by luck.

**Stakes:** The Function Machines control the island's drawbridge. Wrong output = drawbridge stays up = no crossing to Island 4.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Machine 1 — simple rule. Input 3 → Output 7. Input 5 → Output 9. Q1 (warm-up): **What is the rule? Input 10 → Output ?** (Rule: add 4; Output 14). Jake/Blaze writes it as an equation: n + 4 = output.

**Ch2:** Machine 2 — multiplication rule. Input 2 → Output 10. Input 4 → Output 20. Q2: **Rule? Input 6 → ?** (Rule: × 5; Output 30). Q3: **Same machine — work backwards. Output 35 → Input ?** (35 ÷ 5 = 7 — inverse operations)

**Ch3:** Machine 3 — two-step rule. Input 2 → Output 9. Input 4 → Output 13. Q4: **Rule? (×2 then +5).** Verify: 2×2+5=9 ✓. Q5: **Input 7 → Output ?** (7×2+5=19). **Output 25 → Input ?** (25-5=20, 20÷2=10)

**Ch4:** Crooke has accidentally jammed Machine 4. Q6: The jam log shows: "Input unknown → Output 14. Rule: ×3 then −1." **Find the input.** (14+1=15, 15÷3=5). Q7: Another log: "Input unknown → Output 22. Rule: ×4 then +2." **(22-2=20, 20÷4=5 — same input!)**. Q8: Machine 5 rule: "Double the input, then add the input again" = multiply by 3. **Input 8 → Output ?** (24)

**Ch5:** Final machine unlocks the drawbridge. Q9: Two-step: "Input n. Multiply by 2, subtract 3. Output = 11." **Find n.** (n×2−3=11; n×2=14; n=7). Q10 (Boss): "Input n. Add 5, multiply by 3. Output = 30." **Find n.** (n+5=10; n=5). **Verify: (5+5)×3=30 ✓**

**Cliffhanger:** The drawbridge opens. But carved above the gate is a new Scalan symbol: two scales — each with a different unknown. Crooke stares at it and mutters: "There are TWO unknown boxes. That's impossible." Jake/Blaze's eyes light up.

## Issue 4 of 7 — "The Equation Maze"

**Title:** Both Sides Unknown  
**Setting:** Isla Laberinto — an island that IS a maze, with equation locks on every turning point  
**Difficulty:** Year 4 — expressions on both sides, checking solutions by substitution, balance as a strategy

### Story Premise
The Equation Maze covers the entire island. Every fork in the path has an equation lock; wrong answer sends you backwards. Jake/Blaze must navigate without backtracking. Crooke's crew is somewhere in the maze too — their wrong answers keep triggering the "send back" mechanism, and Jake/Blaze can hear them arguing in the distance.

**Stakes:** The treasure map's third piece is at the maze's centre. First one to reach it claims it.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** First intersection. Q1 (warm-up): **5 + n = 12. Is n = 6? Check by substituting.** (5 + 6 = 11 ≠ 12. No. n = 7)

**Ch2:** Rising complexity. Q2: **2 × n = n + 8. What is n?** (n = 8, because 2×8=16 and 8+8=16). Q3: **n + 4 = 2 × n − 2. Find n.** (n = 6: 6+4=10, 2×6-2=10 ✓)

**Ch3:** Jake/Blaze hears Crooke's team hit a dead end. Q4: **3 × n + 1 = n + 9. Find n.** (n=4: 3×4+1=13, 4+9=13 ✓). Q5: **4 × n − 3 = 2 × n + 5. Find n.** (n=4: 16-3=13, 8+5=13 ✓)

**Ch4:** The maze's hardest section — three-way intersection. Q6: **n + 15 = 3 × n + 1. Find n.** (n=7). Q7: **Substitute n=7 into both sides to verify.** (7+15=22; 3×7+1=22 ✓). Q8: A "shortcut" equation: **5 × n − 10 = 0. Find n.** (n=2 — equation equal to zero; a new structure)

**Ch5:** Centre of the maze. Q9: The lock equation: **3 × (n + 2) = 21. Find n.** (n+2=7; n=5). Q10 (Boss): **2 × (n + 3) = n + 9. Find n.** (2n+6=n+9; n=3. Verify: 2×(3+3)=12, 3+9=12 ✓)

**Cliffhanger:** Jake/Blaze gets the map piece — but exits the maze to find Crooke already outside, holding a different map piece she found in the maze wall. She found it accidentally by... substituting the wrong answer and it accidentally worked (a coincidence). She's pleased but confused. "I just... tried a number. Was that the balance thing?" Jake/Blaze sighs.

## Issue 5 of 7 — "The Scales Temple"

**Title:** The Temple of Two Scales  
**Setting:** Isla Templa — an island dominated by the Scalans' greatest temple, with twin balance scale locks on every door  
**Difficulty:** Year 4–5 — equations with larger numbers, multi-step equations, checking solutions

### Story Premise
The Scales Temple is the most sacred Scalan site. Every lock uses two equations simultaneously — the doors only open when both are balanced. Jake/Blaze must crack the double-lock system while also protecting the temple from Crooke, who is starting to understand equations a little better and is becoming a more serious rival.

**Stakes:** The Scalans' library chamber is in this temple — not the final treasure, but a hint to its location. Whoever reads it first gets the directions.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** Temple door 1. Q1 (warm-up): Equation A: **n + 12 = 25. Find n.** (13). Equation B: **13 × 2 = ?** (26). "Both equations connect — the answer from A feeds into B."

**Ch2:** Doors 2 and 3. Q2: **3 × n − 5 = 22. Find n.** (9). Q3: **4 × n + 7 = 43. Find n.** (9 again — same answer! Both unlock the same door from different sides)

**Ch3:** Crooke is trying to brute-force the temple. Q4: **7 × n = 5 × n + 14. Find n.** (7). Q5: A scale weighs things in "mystery units." Left pan: 4 boxes + 5 units. Right pan: 21 units. **4 × n + 5 = 21. Find n.** (n = 4 units per box)

**Ch4:** The inner chamber lock — a sequence of balancing acts. Q6: **n ÷ 4 = 9. Find n.** (36 — division equation with unknown dividend). Q7: **60 ÷ n = 12. Find n.** (5 — unknown divisor). Q8: **n ÷ 3 + 4 = 14. Find n.** (30 — two-step with division)

**Ch5:** The library chamber. Q9: The reading-lock code: **5 × (n − 3) = 20. Find n.** (n−3=4; n=7). Q10 (Boss): **Two equations use different unknowns: a + b = 20. a = 3 × b. Find a and b.** (b=5, a=15 — first two-variable system! Trial, substitution, or working: if a=3b, then 3b+b=20, 4b=20, b=5)

**Cliffhanger:** The library reveals the final location: the Seventh Island. And a warning: "The vault requires two keys — two truths, two balances, two unknowns. One alone cannot open it." Crooke reads over Jake/Blaze's shoulder and pales. She mutters, "I can't do two unknowns."

## Issue 6 of 7 — "Traps and Equations"

**Title:** The Trapped Island  
**Setting:** Isla Trampa — a trap-filled island; every trap is an equation. Step on an unbalanced equation and the trap springs.  
**Difficulty:** Year 4–5 — word problems requiring equation construction, multi-step equations

### Story Premise
The path to the seventh island passes through Isla Trampa. The floor is covered in equation tiles — step on a true equation and it holds firm; step on a false one and it drops away. Jake/Blaze must solve the equations to safely cross. Crooke's crew is following — and stepping on false ones, falling into (non-dangerous but deeply inconvenient) pits.

**Stakes:** Safe passage to the final island. Also: somewhere on this island is the last map piece, which only Crooke has half of.

### Chapters (Summary)

**Ch1:** The first floor of tiles. Q1 (warm-up): Tile says **3 + 4 = 8. True or false?** (False — step off it!). Safe tile: **6 × 4 = 24. True?** (Yes — step on it)

**Ch2:** Moving tiles — they change. Q2: **n + 8 = 17.** The tile asks "n = 8?" **(No — n = 9)**. Q3: Tile shows **4 × n = 36. n = 9?** **(Yes — safe)**

**Ch3:** Crooke falls into Pit 3. Q4: Jake/Blaze reads the label on Crooke's pit trap: **"This trap springs when 2n + 6 = 18 is solved incorrectly." What is the correct n?** (n=6). Q5: **The safe path requires solving: 5n − 4 = 31. Find n.** (n=7)

**Ch4:** Word-problem tiles (equations hidden in sentences). Q6: "A chest has some gold coins. If you add 15, you have 38." **Write the equation and solve.** (n+15=38; n=23). Q7: "A bag has coins. When you multiply by 4, you get 44." **(4n=44; n=11)**. Q8: "Three bags of coins, minus 7, equals 14. How many in each bag?" **(3n−7=14; 3n=21; n=7)**

**Ch5:** The final crossing. Q9: "Some coins are shared equally among 5 pirates. Each gets 9. Minus 3 were given as tax. How many were there before tax?" **(n÷5=9; n=45; 45+3=48 original)**. Q10 (Boss): Word problem — Crooke shouts from her pit: "I have 11 map pieces. I hid 4 on this island. Jake has twice as many as I have now. How many does Jake have?" **(Crooke now: 11−4=7. Jake = 2×7=14)**

**Cliffhanger:** Crooke is still in the pit. Jake/Blaze faces a choice: leave her there (and get ahead) or rescue her (but she has the final map fragment). Jake/Blaze reaches down a hand. Crooke looks shocked. "You're going to help me? I've been stealing from you all arc!" Jake/Blaze: "We need your map piece. And... two unknowns need two people."

## Issue 7 of 7 — "The Final Vault"

**Title:** Two Keys, Two Unknowns  
**Setting:** Isla Final — the seventh island, dominated by the Grand Vault of the Scalans  
**Difficulty:** Year 4–5 — two-variable systems of equations, substitution, multi-step reasoning

### Story Premise
Jake/Blaze and Captain Crooke arrive together at the Grand Vault. The vault door has the most complex Scalan lock ever seen: two scales, each balanced, each with a different unknown weight (a and b). Both equations must be solved simultaneously to find the correct combination. Crooke finally understands that she needs to BALANCE both equations — not just calculate.

**Stakes:** The Scalans' entire library of mathematical knowledge. And Crooke's genuine development as a character — she finally gets what equations mean.

### Chapter Breakdown

**Ch1:** The vault entrance. *Q1:* Review — the vault door shows: **a + b = 10. True when a=4, b=6?** (4+6=10 — yes). **True when a=7, b=4?** (11 ≠ 10 — no). Not every combination works.

**Ch2:** Setting up the system. *Q2:* Clue 1 says: **a + b = 10.** Clue 2 says: **a = b + 2.** Can Jake/Blaze find values that satisfy BOTH? (Try: if b=4, a=6: 6+4=10 ✓ and 6=4+2 ✓ — it works!). *Q3:* **Different system: a + b = 12 and a = 2 × b. Find a and b.** (b=4, a=8)

**Ch3:** Crooke's revelation. She tries to just "pick numbers" and Jake/Blaze shows her the systematic approach. *Q4:* **a − b = 6 and a + b = 14. Find a and b.** (a=10, b=4 — add both equations: 2a=20, a=10). *Q5:* **If a = 3 × b and a + b = 16, find a and b.** (3b+b=16, 4b=16, b=4, a=12). Crooke solves this one herself — first time she's used algebra properly.

**Ch4:** The vault's three locks. Each requires a balanced pair. *Q6:* Lock 1 — **n + 7 = 3 × n − 5. Find n.** (n=6). *Q7:* Lock 2 — **a + b = 18, a = b + 4. Find a and b.** (a=11, b=7). *Q8:* Lock 3 — **Two boxes balance 14 gems on one side. One box + 2 gems balance the other side. How many gems in each box?** (2n=n+2+... let's simplify: 2b=14 when 1b+2=8; b=6 — box = 6 gems, verify: 2×6=12, 6+2=8... adjust: 3b=14+2=wait, simpler version: 2 boxes = 14, so 1 box = 7. One box + 2 = 9. Adjust problem: 2 boxes = 9 + 3 gems means... Simplify for Y4: Two boxes weigh the same as 10 gems. One box weighs the same as 6 gems. **Find what's wrong.** 2×6=12 ≠ 10. The scale isn't balanced — one of the gems is fake! The fake = 12−10 = 2 gems' worth. The mystery weight.)

**Ch5:** The vault opens. *Q9:* Inside is a Scalan calculation: **Treasure items total 150. Divide equally between Jake/Blaze and Crooke. Each gets 1/2. How many?** (75 each). *Q10 (Boss):* The final inscription: **"Balance is when neither side has more. If a = 3b and a + b = 48, find each share."** (3b+b=48; 4b=48; b=12; a=36). Crooke stares at her 12 and Jake/Blaze's 36. "That's... not equal." Jake/Blaze: "No. But it balances. That's different."

**Resolution:** The vault opens to reveal not gold, but thousands of Scalan mathematical tablets — the most advanced mathematics ever discovered, thousands of years old. It's worth more than any treasure. Jake/Blaze arranges for it to be sent to the world's mathematicians. Crooke, surprisingly, asks to study it. "Maybe I should have learned to balance things properly from the start."

## Narrative Tone Notes

**Emotional arc for the reader:**  
Issues 1–2: puzzle excitement (equations as code-cracking — spy energy)  
Issues 3–4: growing mastery (function machines and mazes — game energy)  
Issues 5–6: challenge and moral complexity (working with Crooke — will Jake/Blaze cooperate?)  
Issue 7: genuine triumph and insight (the equals sign is about balance, not answers — the reader really understands this now)

**What makes this world feel distinct:**  
Treasure hunting has a classic adventure energy — maps, traps, islands, rivals. But unlike swashbuckling treasure hunts, the challenge is intellectual, not physical. You can't force the locks — you have to understand them. The Scalan civilisation makes equations feel ancient and important, not school-ish. Captain Crooke is the perfect foil: someone who approaches math the wrong way (calculate, don't balance) and whose failure is instructive.

**Key recurring elements:**  
- The golden balance scale (iconic visual symbol — appears on every lock)
- Captain Crooke and her crew (grows across all 7 issues; character arc from antagonist to reluctant ally)
- Scalan inscriptions (each one a clue and a math problem)
- Map fragments (7 pieces, one per island — physical throughline)
- The phrase "Balance is Truth" (Scalan motto — appears at the start and the resolution)
